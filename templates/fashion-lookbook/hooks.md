# Fashion lookbook — opening hooks

The first 2 seconds decide whether the viewer scrolls past or stays. Fashion hooks are visual, fast, and almost wordless. Pick one that matches the category and commit — never mix two hooks in one cut.

Each hook below has: setup, model direction, camera cue, and the categories it works for.

---

## 1. Mirror / reflection reveal

**Setup.** Model's reflection appears in a mirror, polished window, or still water before the model itself is in frame. Camera holds on the reflection for 1s, then either pans to the real model or the model turns to "meet" their reflection.

**Model direction.** Slow turn, eyes find the mirror first, then camera. Subtle expression — composed, unhurried.

**Camera cue.** Static then slow pan, OR static-mirror-only with model entering frame from edge.

**Best for.** Formal wear, evening / party, accessories, editorial / haute couture.

**Avoid for.** Streetwear (too slow), kids (too cerebral), athleisure (wrong rhythm).

---

## 2. Power-walk straight at camera

**Setup.** Model is mid-stride, walking directly at camera at moderate-to-fast pace. Frame starts at full body, model dominates frame by ~1.5s. Camera at chest height or slightly low looking up.

**Model direction.** Confident strut, shoulders back, head high, gaze locked on lens. Optional: hand in pocket, hand adjusting jacket lapel, sunglasses on.

**Camera cue.** Static or very slow pull-back. NEVER push in — the model does the work.

**Best for.** Streetwear, athleisure, formal/business, contemporary.

**Avoid for.** Romantic / cottagecore (wrong attitude), haute couture (too aggressive — use mirror or curtain).

---

## 3. Slow-motion turn / 360° spin

**Setup.** Model standing still in environment. Music drop or shutter snap. Slow-motion 180° or 360° rotation. Hair lifts, fabric flares, garment catches light from every angle.

**Model direction.** Initiate the spin from the hips, arms relaxed and let them swing naturally. Hair and fabric should lead the motion. Hold final position 0.5s.

**Camera cue.** Static, framing held tight enough to crop feet (mid-thigh up) so the rotation reads.

**Best for.** Evening / party, dresses with movement (chiffon / silk / sequins), haute couture, vintage.

**Avoid for.** Tailored suits / structured silhouettes (no movement to show), kids (proportions read awkward).

---

## 4. Fabric macro pull-back

**Setup.** Extreme close-up on fabric weave — silk shimmer, denim texture, knit ribbing, sequin grid. Frame is abstract for 1s. Camera pulls back over 1s to reveal: it's a sleeve, a hem, a shoulder. Final reveal at 2s shows the model.

**Model direction.** Static or slight breath-movement during the macro hold. Animate (small turn, hand-lift) as the camera reaches medium framing.

**Camera cue.** Smooth zoom-out / dolly-back, never jerky. Match the fabric's character — slow for silk, slightly snappier for denim.

**Best for.** Sustainable, haute couture, fabric-forward campaigns (knitwear brand, silk specialist), accessories (leather goods).

**Avoid for.** Fast-paced multi-look reels — the macro hook eats too much runway.

---

## 5. Curtain / door reveal

**Setup.** Model behind sheer curtain, frosted glass, or doorway. Curtain pulls (practical or motorized), door swings, light spills in. Model emerges or is revealed in full silhouette → full color.

**Model direction.** Begin in silhouette pose, hold absolutely still through the reveal. After full reveal, single beat of stillness, then walk forward.

**Camera cue.** Static, or very slow push-in through the threshold.

**Best for.** Haute couture, evening / party, collection launches, dramatic pieces (gowns, statement coats, fur).

**Avoid for.** Casual / everyday wear (too theatrical), athleisure.

---

## 6. Accessory / detail-first

**Setup.** Macro on shoes hitting pavement, hand with rings adjusting cuff, sunglasses being placed on, bag clasp clicking. Hold the macro 1.5s. Pull back at 2s to reveal full model.

**Model direction.** Hand and foot motion is the lead — make it deliberate. Face often arrives after the 2s mark.

**Camera cue.** Locked macro for the hold, then dolly-back or rack-focus to reveal full body.

**Best for.** Accessories category (shoes / bags / jewelry), streetwear (sneaker hook), formal (cufflink / watch hook).

**Avoid for.** Bodysuit / dress focus where the garment IS the story.

---

## 7. Silhouette shadow

**Setup.** Model's shadow on a wall or pavement before the model itself. Shadow moves — walks, spins, gestures. Real model enters frame at 1.5-2s.

**Model direction.** Movement that reads as silhouette (broad strokes — walk, arm-lift, head-tilt). Once in frame, snap into a confident pose.

**Camera cue.** Static, with shadow occupying 60% of frame.

**Best for.** Streetwear, contemporary, vintage (sepia tones), editorial / artistic campaigns.

**Avoid for.** Soft / romantic lighting categories — shadow hooks need contrast.

---

## 8. Color-coordinated environment match

**Setup.** Model's outfit is the same hue as the wall, set, or natural surround. Hook is the visual rhyme — eye reads silhouette before garment detail.

**Model direction.** Stillness, then gentle movement into a turn or step. Lean into the chromatic cohesion.

**Camera cue.** Wide locked frame, model centered. Hold for the full 2s to let the color match register.

**Best for.** Minimalist fashion, editorial, mood-driven content, monochrome collections.

**Avoid for.** Multi-color collections, eclectic / vintage.

---

## 9. Hair and fabric wind

**Setup.** Model stationary or in slow walk. Strong wind (practical fan or implied) lifts hair and fabric from t=0. Slow-motion. Sensual, dramatic, fashion-film energy.

**Model direction.** Unhurried, eyes either down or to the lens. Hand may move to face / hair / collar in slow motion. Don't fight the wind — let it carry.

**Camera cue.** Static or very gentle dolly. Slow-motion frame rate (60fps interpreted as half-speed).

**Best for.** Evening wear, haute couture, luxury campaigns, fragrance-adjacent lookbooks.

**Avoid for.** Athleisure, kids, accessories (wind hides the accessory).

---

## 10. Rapid flash-cut outfit montage

**Setup.** 3-5 outfit angles in 1 second. Each shot 0.2-0.3s. No transition effects. Cuts straight on the music kick. By 2s the viewer has seen the breadth of the collection.

**Model direction.** Each shot is a held pose. Model resets fully between shots (don't try to fake continuity).

**Camera cue.** Locked frame, identical framing across cuts so the outfit is the only variable.

**Best for.** Lookbook reels, capsule collection launches, multi-piece showcases.

**Avoid for.** Single-look videos, dramatic / cinematic campaigns.

---

## 11. Descend / elevator reveal

**Setup.** Model descending stairs, stepping out of an elevator, walking down a ramp, or coming down an escalator. Camera frames from front or three-quarter. Outfit reveals progressively as the model lowers into frame.

**Model direction.** Each step deliberate. Eye contact only at the bottom of the descent. Hand on rail or at side, never in pocket — the descent should read as arrival.

**Camera cue.** Static or slight tilt-up to keep model centered as they descend.

**Best for.** Luxury, formal wear, evening / party, power-fashion.

**Avoid for.** Casual / everyday, kids.

---

## 12. Zoom-in on face → pull-back on outfit

**Setup.** Open on extreme close-up of model's face — eyes, makeup, expression. Hold 1s. Snap zoom-out to full body and outfit at 2s.

**Model direction.** During the close-up, single small motion — slight lift of chin, half-smile, eyes find the lens. After the snap-back, hold a strong full-body pose.

**Camera cue.** Hard snap-zoom (not a gentle dolly). Trust the cut.

**Best for.** Beauty-integrated fashion, evening / party, contemporary editorial, statement makeup + outfit pairings.

**Avoid for.** Sustainable / earthy categories (wrong rhythm), accessories-only.
