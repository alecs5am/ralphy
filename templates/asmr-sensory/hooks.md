# Hooks — asmr-sensory

ASMR opens are quieter than every other format in this pack. The job of the first 1-2 seconds is **not** to shock-stop the scroll — it's to signal "audio matters here, unmute." Once viewers unmute, the format does the rest.

Pick one. Don't stack two. Don't loud-text on top of a quiet open.

---

## 1. "POV: this is the most satisfying [thing]"

**Open shot.** Macro close-up of the subject in resting state (sealed jar, untouched cream surface, sealed parcel). Tiny silent text overlay top-center: `POV: the most satisfying <thing> on your feed`.

**Sound design.** Faint room tone (~0.5s) → first foley beat. No music.

**Niche fit.** Beauty cream, slime, unboxing.

**Why it stops scroll.** The "POV" frame is familiar to TikTok viewers, and "most satisfying" pre-promises the payoff. The viewer commits the next 3 seconds to verify.

---

## 2. Silent-text-only opener — "watch what happens"

**Open shot.** Macro close-up, subject already in motion (a finger about to press into the cream / a knife already touching the chocolate). Silent text overlay: `watch what happens` or `wait for the sound`.

**Sound design.** Total silence for ~0.5s, then the first foley beat lands hard. No music.

**Niche fit.** Anything with a clear motion → sound payoff: chocolate snap, slime stretch, foil rip.

**Why it stops scroll.** Unmuted scrollers hear silence on a video that's clearly visual-active — the dissonance demands they unmute or stop. Either action breaks the scroll.

---

## 3. "no music, just [sound]"

**Open shot.** Macro close-up of the subject. Silent text overlay: `no music. just the sound of <X>` (e.g. `just the sound of cardboard`, `just the sound of cream`).

**Sound design.** Pure foley from frame 1. No music bed at all (NOT even sub-perceptual).

**Niche fit.** Unboxing (cardboard, tape), spa (water pour), beauty (cream squelch).

**Why it stops scroll.** Pre-frames the audio expectation — the viewer is now listening on purpose. Viewers who came for ASMR self-select; viewers who didn't either bounce or convert into ASMR mode.

---

## 4. "ASMR [thing]"

**Open shot.** Macro close-up of the subject. Tiny text overlay top-center: `ASMR <thing>` (e.g. `ASMR cream texture`, `ASMR matcha ritual`, `ASMR jewelry box`). The genre tag IS the hook.

**Sound design.** First foley beat at ~0.3-0.5s. No music.

**Niche fit.** Any. Most reliable cross-category open.

**Why it stops scroll.** Genre-tagging triggers the dedicated ASMR audience and primes the algorithm for the right co-watch graph. Less novel than the others but most reliable.

---

## 5. "the satisfying sound of [X]"

**Open shot.** Slow push-in macro on the subject (1-2s of slow camera move into the texture). Text: `the satisfying sound of <X>`.

**Sound design.** A long held foley beat (e.g. 2s of cream squelch, 2s of cardboard creak). No music.

**Niche fit.** Beauty cream, slime, foam.

**Why it stops scroll.** The text promises a payoff and the slow push delivers anticipation. Anticipation + sound = stop.

---

## 6. Whisper-narration opener — "we begin with X"

**Open shot.** Macro close-up of the subject in resting state. No text overlay.

**Sound design.** Whisper VO at -3dB: `"we begin with <X>"` or `"the first thing you'll notice is the texture"`. Light room tone underneath. No music.

**Niche fit.** Spa, beauty, craft (slime).

**Why it stops scroll.** Whisper voice is rare in the feed and triggers an attentional reflex — viewers lean in (literally turn up the volume). High intent.

---

## 7. "wait for it" + countdown

**Open shot.** Macro close-up of subject pre-motion. Text: `wait for it` (no countdown numbers — the pacing IS the countdown).

**Sound design.** ~1s silence → soft tap → ~1s silence → bigger foley beat at the reveal.

**Niche fit.** Chocolate snap, foil rip, slime stretch.

**Why it stops scroll.** "Wait for it" is a proven open across formats; in ASMR it works because the payoff is sonic, not visual. Differentiates from the visual "wait for it" that's everywhere.

---

## 8. Genre-naming pure foley — "tapping" / "scraping" / "crinkling"

**Open shot.** Extreme macro on the source of the sound (a fingernail about to tap, a spoon about to scrape, a piece of foil about to crinkle). Text: single word — `tapping.` or `scraping.` or `crinkling.`

**Sound design.** Word-perfect foley from frame 1. No music. No VO.

**Niche fit.** Tap-only ASMR (a niche unto itself), unboxing, food.

**Why it stops scroll.** The single-word label is honest and minimal — the viewer knows exactly what they're getting and stays for it. Filters out non-ASMR audience cleanly.

---

## 9. "the moment [X] hits [Y]"

**Open shot.** Macro setup: cream just before it touches skin / chocolate just before the knife / spoon just before the matcha. Text: `the moment <X> hits <Y>`.

**Sound design.** ~0.5-1s silence → contact foley at the labeled moment.

**Niche fit.** Beauty (cream → skin), food (knife → chocolate), spa (water → bowl).

**Why it stops scroll.** Names the exact sonic event the viewer is here for. Maximum specificity = maximum self-selection.

---

## 10. "raw sound. no edits."

**Open shot.** Macro close-up, no text overlay or single tiny line: `raw sound · no edits`.

**Sound design.** Live-recorded diegetic foley from frame 1, very slight room tone (don't gate it down to silence — sounds artificial).

**Niche fit.** Food, craft, unboxing — anywhere "authenticity" is part of the appeal.

**Why it stops scroll.** "No edits" reads as a quality / authenticity flag in an algorithmically-suspicious feed. Viewers reward the claim with attention if the audio actually sounds raw.

---

## 11. "[N] sounds in [M] seconds"

**Open shot.** Macro on subject. Text: `7 sounds in 30 seconds` or `5 textures, 1 minute`.

**Sound design.** Each beat counts as one labeled sound. Optional small counter ticker (1, 2, 3...) in the corner.

**Niche fit.** Multi-beat unboxing, multi-step beauty routine, multi-texture slime.

**Why it stops scroll.** Quantified promise — viewers stay to verify the count. Rewatchability spikes (people replay to count again).

---

## 12. Direct cold-open — no text, no overlay

**Open shot.** Macro close-up. Subject already in mid-motion at frame 1.

**Sound design.** Foley from frame 1. No music. No text. No VO.

**Niche fit.** Returning audience, channel-style content where the genre is already established.

**Why it stops scroll.** Highest-friction option — works only if your channel already signals "ASMR" and the algo has the right co-watch graph. For a brand-new account, prefer #1, #3, or #4.

---

## Sound-design rules across all hooks

1. **Music is OFF in the first 3 seconds.** Even if the rest of the video uses sub-perceptual ambient, hold the music until at least 3s in (or skip it entirely). Music in the first beat fights the foley and kills the format signal.
2. **Loud caption styles are forbidden** in ASMR opens. No HormoziCaptions, no KaraokeCaptions, no big bouncy text. MinimalCaptions only, at low opacity, no entrance animation.
3. **First foley beat lands within 1.5s.** If the viewer waits longer than that for the first sound, they bounce — the audio promise has to deliver fast.
4. **Whispers and silences are valid.** "Quiet open" is a feature, not a bug. Don't reach for an attention-grabber when the format is built for the opposite reflex.
