# Fight Scene Hooks — First 2 Seconds

The opening frame must already be in motion. No "two fighters facing off, then the fight starts" — the viewer scrolls before the kick lands. Each hook below ships an apex pose, a sound cue, and a follow-through.

Pick one. Pasting two hooks back-to-back is a common failure mode: the second hook lands on a viewer who already dropped attention.

---

## 1. Mid-Action Freeze Frame

**Setup.** Cut directly into the apex of a strike. Fist or blade mid-impact, both combatants at maximum extension, muscles coiled. Hold 0.5s in slow-motion, then explode back to full speed.

**Why it works.** Skips all setup. The viewer's brain immediately reverse-engineers the context, which is the most engaged state you can put a viewer in.

**Example.** "Open on a punch frozen 1cm from the defender's jaw. Defender's eyes are wide, sweat beads suspended in the air. Hold for 12 frames at 0.25x. Then snap to 1.0x: the punch lands, head whips back, sweat sprays in an arc."

---

## 2. Weapon Clash with Spark Burst

**Setup.** Two blades collide dead-center screen on frame 1. Orange sparks explode radially. Camera pulls back 30cm with the shockwave.

**Why it works.** The clash is the visual climax of an exchange that hasn't happened yet — viewer fills it in.

**Example.** "Katana and longsword lock in the dead center of the frame, sparks bursting outward in all directions. Both fighters' arms are visibly straining. Camera dollies back smoothly as the sound rings out."

---

## 3. Charge Toward Camera

**Setup.** The aggressor sprints directly at the camera, limbs wild, intensity at 11. Camera shakes from proximity. Cut just before impact.

**Why it works.** Forces the viewer's instinct to flinch — physiological hook.

**Example.** "A masked fighter sprints at the camera through a rain-soaked alley, fists clenched, neon reflecting off their wet jacket. At 1.5s the camera shudders, then hard cut to a wide shot of the actual fight."

---

## 4. Slow-Motion Punch Connect

**Setup.** A perfectly centered punch lands in 0.25x speed. Show contact: skin deforming, head whipping, eyes widening, sweat sheeting off.

**Why it works.** Crystallizes the impact moment. Pure visual cortex food.

**Example.** "Slow-motion fist connects with the defender's cheekbone. Skin ripples outward from the impact point, head rotates 30 degrees, droplets of sweat fly diagonally across frame in a perfect arc."

---

## 5. Ground-Slam Shockwave

**Setup.** Fighter slams fist or body into earth. Visible shockwave radiates outward, kicking dust in a perfect circle. Secondary fighter staggers backward.

**Why it works.** Implies superhuman force without showing it directly. Reads as anime/wuxia/superhero register instantly.

**Example.** "A heavy gauntleted fist smashes into stone. A circular dust shockwave radiates 5 meters outward. Cracks spider-web through the ground. The opponent stumbles back two steps, raising their arm against the dust."

---

## 6. Blade Unsheathing with Light Flash

**Setup.** Draw a katana or longsword in one explosive motion. Blade catches a sun ray or torchlight, brilliant flash. Auditory: long metallic ring resolving to a sharp note.

**Why it works.** Promises the entire fight in one gesture. Samurai/duel register lock-in.

**Example.** "Slow zoom on the swordsman's hand. The katana draws in one fluid motion. The blade catches the golden hour sun and flashes brilliant white across the entire frame. Their opponent involuntarily steps back."

---

## 7. Aerial Descending Kick

**Setup.** Fighter launches vertically, leg extended in axe kick or flying kick. They descend toward the opponent at camera eye-level. Ground impact creates dust on contact.

**Why it works.** Vertical motion is rare in social cuts; immediately distinguishes the cut.

**Example.** "Camera at ground level. A figure descends from above, leg extended in an axe kick. They land hard on a wooden platform — dust and splinters explode outward. The defender raises crossed forearms a millisecond too late."

---

## 8. Dual-Wield Twirl into Power Stance

**Setup.** Character spins twin weapons (swords, staffs, nunchaku, sai) in rapid succession. Blades blur. Stops in a power stance, weapons crossed across the chest.

**Why it works.** Pure intent signal. Tells the viewer "this fighter is technically dangerous" before any contact.

**Example.** "A dual-wielder spins two short swords in figure-eight patterns, blades blurring. After 1.2s they snap into a low stance, both blades crossed in front of their chest, eyes locked on the off-screen opponent."

---

## 9. Whip / Chain Snap Through Frame

**Setup.** Whip or chain weapon snaps through the frame with violent acceleration. Auditory crack required.

**Why it works.** Long-range weapons are visually rare; the snap reads as both threat and motion blur in one beat.

**Example.** "A heavy chain whip snaps from screen-left to screen-right at the frame's midpoint, the tip cutting a deep groove into a stone pillar in the background. Stone chips fly. The wielder is half in frame, anchoring the motion."

---

## 10. Explosive Sidestep / Dodge

**Setup.** One combatant charges. The other pivots and sidesteps explosively. Attacker's momentum carries them past camera; chase-cam follows the dodge.

**Why it works.** Inverts expectation — viewer thinks they're seeing an attack land, then realizes the defender is the protagonist.

**Example.** "A heavy spear thrust comes at the camera. At the last frame the camera whip-pans 45 degrees as the defender slips sideways. The spear-wielder's momentum carries them past frame; camera locks onto the defender's grin."

---

## 11. Pre-Impact Silence + Held Breath

**Setup.** Opening 1.5s is near-silent. Both fighters frozen mid-stance, locked eyes, breathing visible in cold air. Then sudden explosion of motion and sound on frame 2.0s.

**Why it works.** Audio contrast — silence is a hook in a feed of music-saturated content.

**Example.** "Two fighters lock eyes across a snowfield. Their breath plumes in cold air. Wind is the only sound. At 1.8s the swordmaster's eye twitches. At 2.0s both explode forward, blades drawn — full orchestral hit lands on contact."

---

## 12. Environmental Cue Hook (Bamboo Split, Glass Crack)

**Setup.** First visual is the environment reacting — a bamboo stalk splitting clean, a window spider-cracking, a wall denting — _before_ revealing the fighters. Camera then pulls back to show what caused it.

**Why it works.** Mystery hook. The viewer sees the consequence before the cause.

**Example.** "A clean bamboo stalk splits diagonally and falls. Camera pulls back fast to reveal a swordsman mid-follow-through, opponent already rolling away. Petals and bamboo leaves drift through the frame."

---

## Hook ranking (reliability for short-form)

For 9:16 social, in order of best-to-worst stop-rate based on how cleanly the model renders them:

1. Slow-motion punch connect (#4)
2. Mid-action freeze frame (#1)
3. Aerial descending kick (#7)
4. Weapon clash with spark burst (#2)
5. Blade unsheathing with light flash (#6)
6. Ground-slam shockwave (#5)
7. Dual-wield twirl (#8)
8. Charge toward camera (#3)
9. Explosive sidestep (#10)
10. Whip/chain snap (#9)
11. Environmental cue (#12)
12. Pre-impact silence (#11) — high quality but lower stop-rate, use only when the content has time to breathe
