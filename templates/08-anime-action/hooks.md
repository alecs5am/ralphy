# Anime Action — Hooks

> The opening 2 seconds of an anime clip do one job: instantly establish the anime aesthetic and lock attention. Pick **one** hook per video. Do not stack two — they fight each other and dilute the read. Every hook below is a complete recipe (visual + timing + lighting + sound) ready to drop into the `[OPENING 2-SECOND HOOK]` block of the master template in `prompt-cookbook.md`.

Each entry: **what it is → exact 0:00–0:02 description → genre fit → lighting / color / sound notes**.

---

## 1. Dramatic Eye Close-Up (light reflection)

The signature anime opening. An extreme close-up of a single eye that fills 60% of the frame. Iris is non-realistic (gold, violet, ringed cyan). Multi-layered highlights — a four-pointed star or cross-shape — sit inside the iris. Hold still 0.5 s, then pupil contracts or dilates and the camera pulls back to reveal the character at 1.0–1.5 s.

- **Fit:** shounen, seinen, mecha, isekai, supernatural school. Universal opener.
- **Light / color:** dramatic rim or directional light from a single side. Iris highlight is white or cyan-white. Saturation 120–140%.
- **Sound:** 0.0–0.3 s ambient / silence. 0.3 s a single ascending tone or piano note. Orchestral sting at 1.0 s when pupil contracts.

## 2. Speed-Line Burst

Straight perspective lines (sharp, not soft) radiate from a center point or trail behind a swooping object. Lines fill 70% of the frame. Combine with a slight zoom-in or an object entering. Lines clear by 1.5 s revealing the scene.

- **Fit:** shounen action, sports, OP montage, action set-piece.
- **Light / color:** high contrast, single bright key. Lines are typically white or a deep accent color (black on white background, or white on color). Saturation 130%+.
- **Sound:** zippy whoosh at 0.0 s, orchestral or J-rock guitar stab at 1.0 s when lines clear.

## 3. Transformation Flash

Frame goes white over 0.2 s (pure white overlay, fully obscuring), holds white for 0.3 s (peak overload), then pulls back over 0.5–1.0 s revealing the character in a new state — different outfit, powered-up form, color-shifted aura.

- **Fit:** magical girl, shounen power-up, isekai class-up reveal, OP transition between cuts.
- **Light / color:** the flash itself reads as overexposed white; on pull-back, accent color (pink for magical girl, gold for shounen, cyan for mecha) saturates the new state.
- **Sound:** ascending crystalline chime / bell during 0.0–0.3 s buildup, then a ringing impact stinger and music drop at the reveal.

## 4. Blade Unsheath with Metal Gleam

A sword (or staff, scythe, spear) being drawn from a sheath. The instant the blade clears the sheath at ~0.6 s, a brilliant white reflection flashes across its length, traveling tip-to-hilt over 0.2 s. Slight slow-motion on the gleam. Hold the drawn-blade pose 0.4 s before any further motion.

- **Fit:** shounen, seinen samurai, isekai, mecha (energy weapon variant), action OP.
- **Light / color:** dark backdrop with one bright rim source so the gleam pops. Blade is desaturated steel; gleam is pure white. Background can be golden-hour, blood-red, or cool blue depending on tone.
- **Sound:** the iconic *shing* — high metallic ring at 0.6 s, a quiet half-second of held tension after, then orchestra returns.

## 5. Power-Up Aura Explosion

Aura erupts outward from a character's silhouette in concentric rings of glowing energy. Three to five rings expand and dissipate over 1.5 s. Particles trail upward inside the aura. Background distorts at the aura's edge (heat-shimmer / air-pressure ripple). Hair lifts impossibly upward.

- **Fit:** shounen climax, magical girl, mecha core ignition, isekai spell-cast.
- **Light / color:** color is genre-coded — golden = hero, cyan / blue = ki / ice, red / orange = rage / fire, purple = forbidden / royal, pink = magical girl, white = holy. Rim-lights everything. Saturation 140%.
- **Sound:** sub-bass rumble + ascending electronic charge tone 0.0–1.2 s, orchestral brass sting on the outermost ring at 1.5 s.

## 6. Cherry Blossom Wind Gust

Semi-translucent pink/white sakura petals drift across the frame on invisible wind. Petals are sharp-focused; the background is heavily blurred. Camera holds still or pulls back very slowly. Behind the petals, a silhouette or out-of-focus character / window slowly resolves at 1.5 s.

- **Fit:** romance, drama, slice-of-life, emotional farewell, ED-style outro, school-anime opener.
- **Light / color:** golden-hour warm or blue-hour cool. Petals catch a single soft light. Saturation 70–85% (intentionally muted). Shadows are warm brown or cool blue, never black.
- **Sound:** soft acoustic guitar or solo piano holding a single chord. Subtle wind. No percussion. Silence is acceptable and powerful.

## 7. Anime Title-Card Slam

Bold, thick-outlined title text (English, katakana, or kanji) rotates into the center of the frame from off-screen with a perspective effect, settling at 0:01.5. Text has a dark outline and a gradient fill (gold/cyan, red/black, white/pink). Speed lines trail behind the text as it lands. A subtitle or kanji-mark may appear under it 0.3 s later.

- **Fit:** OP / opening sequence, episode-style hook, hype reveal, montage intro.
- **Light / color:** dark sky or silhouette background so the title pops. Title gradient is high-contrast.
- **Sound:** a low *whoosh* during entry, a sharp orchestral / cymbal *crash* at the slam-stop. J-rock guitar enters immediately after.

## 8. Silhouette Against Dramatic Sky

A character (or mecha, or single object) stands in pure black silhouette against a sky filling 60–70% of the frame. Sky is one of: golden sunset, deep blue twilight, storm-cloud purple, or sakura-pink dawn. The silhouette is razor-sharp, not feathered. Slow-zoom over 1.5 s to push presence.

- **Fit:** seinen drama, mecha hero shot, isekai establishing, ED outro, character intro card.
- **Light / color:** the sky is the color story. Saturation 100–130% in the sky, with the silhouette's edge crisp and dark. Optional: a thin rim-light on the silhouette in the sky's dominant color.
- **Sound:** sustained orchestral string note (one chord) building slowly. Wind ambient layered underneath.

## 9. Impact Frame with Cross-Highlight

The video opens *mid-impact*. A frozen moment of contact (fist meeting jaw, blade meeting blade, body meeting wall) with a bright white cross-shaped highlight at the contact point and surrounding area motion-blurred backward (smear frame around the static center). Hold 0.3 s. Then time resumes and the rest of the scene plays.

- **Fit:** shounen climax, sports, mecha clash, fight scene set-piece.
- **Light / color:** the cross is overexposed white. Surrounding palette can be saturated or desaturated; the cross dominates. Saturation 130%+.
- **Sound:** total silence at 0:00.0 — *no audio at all* for 0.2 s — then a massive orchestral CRASH (full brass + low bass) at the moment time resumes. Pre-impact silence is the load-bearing trick.

## 10. Chibi Reaction Pop

The character flashes briefly into chibi proportions (oversized head, tiny body, exaggerated expression — surprise / anger / blush). Lasts 0.4 s, accompanied by a comedic pulse-glow and a screen-tone halftone overlay. Then snap-cuts back to normal proportions or to the next beat.

- **Fit:** romance-comedy, slice-of-life, sports comic relief, magical girl. Never shounen-serious or seinen.
- **Light / color:** warm pastel, saturation 90%, blush-pink accents. Screen-tone overlay diagonal-line or dot-pattern.
- **Sound:** comedic *boink* or light *pop* synth, optional cartoon stretch sound.

## 11. Sweat-Drop / Anger-Vein Pop

A single glossy bead of sweat appears on the character's temple (nervousness, effort) — or a thick purple anger-vein cluster pulses on the forehead (rage). Both render at large size, catch a directional highlight, and hold 0.5 s before the camera pulls back.

- **Fit:** romance-comedy, slice-of-life, sports tension, light shounen. Sweat = nervous; vein = comically angry.
- **Light / color:** warm everyday palette. The drop / vein is rendered with hard highlights. Saturation 85%.
- **Sound:** soft *plip* (sweat) or a rising tense string note (vein). Comic *boink* if going full-comedy.

## 12. Screen-Tone / Manga Overlay Flash

The whole frame momentarily overlays with a halftone-dot pattern or diagonal manga line-pattern, with the underlying scene partially visible through it. Lasts 0.2–0.4 s, then clears. Used at the start of an emotional or comedic peak. Often paired with a pose freeze.

- **Fit:** seinen psychological beat, romance-comedy emotional spike, slice-of-life comedic moment, drama emphasis.
- **Light / color:** pattern is high-contrast black-on-color or color-on-black. Underlying scene's saturation drops 20% during overlay.
- **Sound:** sharp single-note string stab, or dramatic silence (chosen by tone).

## Picking the right hook

| Brief / vibe | Default hook |
|---|---|
| Shounen climax / battle / power-up | 5 (Aura Explosion) or 1 (Eye Close-Up) |
| Sports / OP montage / hype | 2 (Speed-Line Burst) or 7 (Title Slam) |
| Seinen drama / introspection | 1 (Eye Close-Up) or 8 (Silhouette) |
| Magical girl / transformation | 3 (Transformation Flash) |
| Mecha hero shot | 8 (Silhouette) or 5 (Aura — for core ignition) |
| Sword / samurai / weapon reveal | 4 (Blade Unsheath) |
| Romance / emotional farewell | 6 (Sakura Wind) |
| Mecha clash / combat opener | 9 (Impact Frame) |
| Romance-comedy / cute moment | 10 (Chibi Pop) or 11 (Sweat Drop) |
| Slice-of-life morning | 6 (Sakura Wind) |
| Dramatic emotional peak | 12 (Screen-Tone Flash) |

When in doubt: **1 (Eye Close-Up)** is the safest universal anime opener.
