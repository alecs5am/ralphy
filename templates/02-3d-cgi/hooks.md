# Hooks — 3D / CGI

The hook is the first 1-2 seconds of the clip. CGI without a strong hook reads as a screensaver. Pick one technique below, write it as the opening sentence of your prompt, then build the rest of the prompt around it.

**Rule:** the hook describes what the viewer sees in the first frame and what changes by the second frame. It is visual and actionable, not abstract.

---

## 1. Dramatic Camera Fly-Through

**Setup.** Camera moves through impossible geometry at high speed — crystalline tunnel, organic cathedral, fractal corridor. Walls flow past.

**Why it works.** Forced motion grabs the eye. The viewer can't help but follow the path. Strong for sci-fi, abstract, architectural reveals.

**Example.** "Camera hurtles through a crystalline tunnel, light refracting into fractal patterns on the walls."

---

## 2. Scale Reveal (Tiny → Massive)

**Setup.** Open on extreme close-up of something microscopic — a water droplet, a grain of sand, a single fiber. Pull back to reveal it's part of something enormous.

**Why it works.** Scale shifts trigger awe. The viewer reframes their understanding mid-clip.

**Example.** "Begin on a single raindrop on a leaf. Pull back to reveal a forest canopy in a thunderstorm."

---

## 3. Impossible Camera Angle

**Setup.** Camera occupies a position no real camera can — inside glass, on water surface tension, rotating through a solid cross-section, orbiting at impossible speed.

**Why it works.** Signals "this is CGI" in a good way. Buys creative latitude the viewer accepts.

**Example.** "Camera rotates around a spinning hourglass, passing through its glass surface to show sand falling in real time."

---

## 4. Object Materializing from Particles

**Setup.** Thousands of small particles (dust, light, pixels, data) swirl in formation and coalesce into a recognizable form.

**Why it works.** Anticipation + reveal in a single beat. Universally compelling.

**Example.** "Golden particles swirl and coalesce into the shape of a dancing figure, settling into a detailed humanoid form."

---

## 5. Photorealistic Object in Surreal Setting

**Setup.** Hyper-real subject (luxury product, photographic-quality figure) sits in an impossible environment — floating on clouds, suspended in void, surrounded by aurora.

**Why it works.** Cognitive dissonance creates instant intrigue. Standard for luxury brand work.

**Example.** "A pristine crystal crown sits alone on a floor of clouds while an aurora borealis swirls overhead."

---

## 6. Transformative Morph

**Setup.** One object smoothly transitions into another, defying physics. Marble → silver sphere → water droplet.

**Why it works.** Continuous transformation is hypnotic. Viewer waits for the next state.

**Example.** "A marble rolls across white space, morphing mid-motion into a silver sphere, then into a water droplet."

---

## 7. Reveal Through Transparency

**Setup.** Solid opaque object becomes transparent or cracks open, revealing internal structure. Stone statue → glowing molten core.

**Why it works.** Hidden-becomes-visible is one of the oldest narrative beats. Works at any duration.

**Example.** "A stone statue cracks, light pouring through fissures. The exterior dissolves to reveal a glowing core of molten light within."

---

## 8. Depth-of-Field Shift

**Setup.** Open with extreme DOF — most of frame blurred. Shift focus to reveal what's actually there.

**Why it works.** The eye instinctively follows focus changes. Strong for product reveals.

**Example.** "Focus on a dust particle suspended in light. Shift focus to reveal a vast interior architecture behind it."

---

## 9. Chromatic Refraction

**Setup.** Light bends, splits, or refracts through a prism / crystal / lens, creating rainbow effects and optical distortion.

**Why it works.** Optical phenomena read as luxurious. Default for high-end product, jewelry, tech.

**Example.** "A prism catches sunlight, fragmenting the entire frame into a spectrum. The light gradually refracts around the product."

---

## 10. Kinetic Decomposition

**Setup.** A structured object (watch, building, sculpture) disassembles into floating components, revealing internal mechanisms.

**Why it works.** Curiosity hook — viewer wants to see how it works. Classic for technical / engineered subjects.

**Example.** "A mechanical watch's face dissolves into hundreds of floating components, each rotating slowly, revealing intricate gears and springs."

---

## 11. Environmental Reaction

**Setup.** Subject enters an environment that reacts physically — water ripples, particles scatter, wind displaces, caustics shift.

**Why it works.** Physical interaction sells the realism. The world responds, so the world is real.

**Example.** "A hand slowly emerges from water. Concentric ripples radiate outward. Caustics dance across the palm and fingers."

---

## 12. Temporal Speed Manipulation

**Setup.** Alternates between slow-motion and fast-motion in the same clip, or shows the same motion at two speeds.

**Why it works.** Time distortion is inherently cinematic. Highlights detail the viewer would otherwise miss.

**Example.** "A flower blooms in extreme slow-motion — petal unfurling takes 3 seconds. Jump to fast-motion: an entire garden blooming in 2 seconds."

---

## Picking a hook

| Subject | Strongest hooks |
|---|---|
| Luxury product | 5 (surreal setting), 9 (refraction), 8 (DOF shift), 4 (particle materialization) |
| Character / creature | 4 (particle materialization), 7 (transparency reveal), 11 (environmental reaction) |
| Architecture / environment | 1 (fly-through), 2 (scale reveal), 3 (impossible angle) |
| Tech / engineered object | 10 (kinetic decomposition), 6 (morph), 9 (refraction) |
| Abstract / artistic | 6 (morph), 12 (temporal manipulation), 3 (impossible angle), 4 (particle materialization) |
| Food / organic | 11 (environmental reaction), 2 (scale reveal), 8 (DOF shift) |

**Default if uncertain:** 4 (particle materialization) for character/product, 1 (fly-through) for environment, 9 (refraction) for luxury.
