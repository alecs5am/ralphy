# Hooks — GRWM

Twelve opener patterns for the first 0-3 seconds of a GRWM. Each one fires both layers simultaneously — a story-pulling line over a visual that establishes the "getting ready" frame. Pick by occasion + narrative type. Do not copy verbatim; rewrite around the user's actual story.

The setup column says what the line does. The niche fit says where it lands hardest. The audio cue says how the VO should sound on delivery — feed this into the ElevenLabs call.

---

## 1. "GRWM for [event]"

> "GRWM for the strangest date I've been on this year."

- **Setup.** Names the format and the occasion in one beat. The viewer immediately knows the genre and gets a story-tease.
- **Niche fit.** Universal — beauty, fashion, lifestyle. Default opener if nothing else fits.
- **Audio cue.** Conversational, slight upward inflection on "this year." Not breathless — confident.

## 2. "Today I'm [verb-ing], so let me tell you about [thing]"

> "Today I'm getting ready for my best friend's wedding, so let me tell you why I'm not bringing my boyfriend."

- **Setup.** The "so" connects the visual track to the narrative track explicitly. Permission to drift between them.
- **Niche fit.** Storytime-heavy GRWM. Beauty + lifestyle.
- **Audio cue.** Casual mid-sentence pivot on "so." Light, conspiratorial.

## 3. "Strangest [event] of my life…"

> "Today I'm having the strangest date of my life — let me tell you while I do my base."

- **Setup.** Pure curiosity gap. The viewer doesn't know the story yet, only that it's worth staying for. The "while I do my base" stitches the second layer.
- **Niche fit.** Gossip, hot-take, confession. Beauty.
- **Audio cue.** Drop on "of my life" — almost confessional. Then a quieter "let me tell you" like leaning into the camera.

## 4. "GRWM while I tell you about [story]"

> "GRWM for work, while I tell you about the email my boss sent me at 2am."

- **Setup.** Explicit contract: visual = getting ready, audio = story. Lowest cognitive cost for the viewer.
- **Niche fit.** Corporate GRWM, "GRWM for an investor meeting", office storytime.
- **Audio cue.** Slightly weary on "boss sent me at 2am." Earned eye-roll energy.

## 5. "I have [X minutes/hours] before [event]"

> "I have 40 minutes before my flight and I still need to do my whole face."

- **Setup.** Time pressure becomes the visual rhythm. Justifies the jump-cuts ("she's rushing").
- **Niche fit.** Travel, fitness ("20 min before the gym"), busy-mom lifestyle.
- **Audio cue.** Slightly clipped, urgent but not panicked. Energy at 7/10.

## 6. "POV: you have [event] and [complication]"

> "POV: you have a first date and you spilled coffee on the only outfit that fits."

- **Setup.** POV-framed hook. The viewer is dropped into the situation immediately.
- **Niche fit.** Comedic GRWM, Gen-Z lifestyle, fashion.
- **Audio cue.** Played slightly deadpan — the comedy is in the under-reaction.

## 7. "Get ready with me but I'm [unusual state]"

> "Get ready with me but I'm running on three hours of sleep."

- **Setup.** Adds an immediate twist that justifies a longer storytime ("here's why I didn't sleep").
- **Niche fit.** Confession, vlog-update. Beauty + lifestyle.
- **Audio cue.** Genuine fatigue in the voice — slightly slower, lower pitch. Stability 0.5 instead of 0.4.

## 8. "GRWM for the [unusual occasion]"

> "GRWM for the meeting where I'm asking for a 30% raise."

- **Setup.** Niche occasion = niche audience locked in. Filters for the people who care about this exact scenario.
- **Niche fit.** Corporate GRWM, advice content. Investor-meeting / interview / pitch GRWM.
- **Audio cue.** Slightly tighter than default — "I'm doing this on purpose" energy.

## 9. "I'm [doing X], you're [coming along]"

> "I'm going to a wedding I don't want to go to, you're coming with me."

- **Setup.** Direct address ("you're coming with me") frames the viewer as the friend in the bathroom mirror. High-trust opener.
- **Niche fit.** Lifestyle, friendship-forward beauty creators.
- **Audio cue.** Like talking to a single specific person, not a camera. Eye contact in the visual.

## 10. "Hot take while I [do step]"

> "Hot take while I do my eyeliner — situationships are worse than relationships."

- **Setup.** Promises a take. The audio track is now the main pull; visual is the rhythm.
- **Niche fit.** Opinion / commentary creators. Lifestyle, dating, pop-culture.
- **Audio cue.** Conversational but pointed. Slight emphasis on the punchword ("worse").

## 11. "Storytime: [setup]. GRWM-style."

> "Storytime: I think my coworker is in love with me. GRWM-style because I have a meeting in 20 minutes."

- **Setup.** Leans into the storytime contract. The "GRWM-style" feels like a wink at the format.
- **Niche fit.** Corporate, gossip, confessional. Crossover audience between storytime channels and GRWM channels.
- **Audio cue.** Mid-volume, slightly faster than default. The story is the reason; the makeup is the prop.

## 12. "Trying [X] for the first time, GRWM"

> "Trying Rare Beauty's new blush for the first time, GRWM for brunch."

- **Setup.** Honest product trial framed as routine. Native placement that doesn't read as an ad.
- **Niche fit.** Beauty, native product placement. Sephora / Rare Beauty / Charlotte Tilbury collabs.
- **Audio cue.** Curious, slightly hesitant on "first time" — earned skepticism. Pays off when the final look reveals.

---

## Picking a hook

- **Default:** #1 ("GRWM for [event]") — lowest risk, universal.
- **High curiosity / gossip:** #3, #10, #11.
- **Comedic / Gen-Z:** #6, #7.
- **Corporate / advice:** #4, #8.
- **Native product placement:** #12.
- **Storytime-heavy:** #2, #4, #11.

Whatever you pick, **the first 3 seconds must contain both layers** — a story tease + a visual that establishes the getting-ready frame. A hook that only fires one layer underperforms the format average.
