# Hooks — Noski Deadpan 2-Hander

The 0-3s opening beat for the deadpan-philosophical 2-hander. Unlike fast-scroll formats, this register holds the viewer with a single static frame + the *promise* of a half-rhetorical question — not with kinetic typography or a punch-in. The hook is the *composition*, not a caption.

## How to read this file

Each hook lists:
- **First-frame anchor** — which template asset / scene-anchor type opens the piece.
- **Audio profile (BPM range)** — usually silence or a slow fade-in of the lofi bed.
- **Vibe note** — what the establishing shot has to do.
- **Source-project fit** — whether the source project used this hook.

If you find yourself writing on-screen text for the hook, stop — the format works without captions. Adding hook captions pushes the piece into "ad" pattern-recognition.

---

## 1. Wide establishing two-shot (the validated default)

- **First-frame anchor:** `location-master-plate.png` — both characters seated head-back on the couch, full room visible, eye-level medium-wide front.
- **Audio profile:** silence for 0-0.8s, then lofi bed fades in over 1.5s at -22 LUFS, BPM 65.
- **Vibe note:** the frame holds for 1.5-2.5s with no dialog. Both bodies are still, eyes up to the ceiling. The viewer sees the entire premise in one frame before anyone speaks. The first line lands on top of the established silence around 2-3s in.
- **Source-project fit:** yes — this is exactly how `noski-people-001/final-v4.mp4` opens (held wide for ~2.5s before Speaker B's first question lands).

## 2. Top-down on one face, dialog lands cold

- **First-frame anchor:** a `cu-character-topdown` close-up — single face filling 70% of frame, eyes up to the camera lens.
- **Audio profile:** silence for 0-0.5s, then VO lands cold. Music bed enters later (around 5-6s) on the cut to the wide.
- **Vibe note:** intimate, intense. The viewer sees one face at extreme proximity, eyes locked to camera. The deadpan delivery hits before the viewer knows there's a second person. The reveal of the wide happens at scene 2 or 3.
- **Source-project fit:** alternate opening considered but not used. Faster scroll-stop than hook 1 — better for shorter-attention platforms (Reels, Shorts) than for TikTok where 2-3s of held wide is fine.

## 3. Story-driven detail cutaway as cold open

- **First-frame anchor:** a `detail-*` cutaway (sock on rug, framed blurred-face photo, dust motes in window light, ticking brass clock, hand with ring at rest).
- **Audio profile:** silence + faint room tone for 0-0.8s, then the lofi bed enters under the first dialog line.
- **Vibe note:** the detail object becomes a metaphor that the dialog will *resolve*. Open on a sock half-tucked under the rug, then cut to the wide where Speaker B asks "Socks or people — what gets lost more often?" The metaphor lands twice — once as image, once as line.
- **Source-project fit:** the source project used `detail-sock-rug.png` as scene-04 (not the cold open) — but the pattern works as a hook in a future variant.

## 4. Faint sound + black frame fade-in (atmospheric)

- **First-frame anchor:** a half-second of black, then fade-in to `location-master-plate.png` at 0-0.5s.
- **Audio profile:** the lofi bed is the first thing the viewer hears, faintly, before the image resolves. By 1s the bed is at full -22 LUFS volume; the frame is fully visible; the first line lands at 2-3s.
- **Vibe note:** cinematic, melancholic. Sells the "indie short film" register hard. Skippable on TikTok (scroll instinct kicks in during black). Use on Reels / Shorts / YouTube where 0.5s of patience is normal.
- **Source-project fit:** not used. Worth trying for a future variant on platforms with slower scroll cadence.

## 5. Mid-conversation cold open (no setup)

- **First-frame anchor:** a side-profile close-up of Speaker A or B, already mid-sentence.
- **Audio profile:** VO is already in progress on frame 1 — first word lands at 0.0-0.2s. Music bed under the VO from the start.
- **Vibe note:** "you're walking into a conversation already happening." Skip the establishing wide entirely — the deadpan register is so dense per beat that the viewer can fill in the premise in 2-3 lines. Riskier — works if the opening line is self-contained, fails if context is needed.
- **Source-project fit:** not used. Pattern worth testing for very short (24-scene, ~50s) variants.

---

## Which hook to pick

- **Default / TikTok-first / 73-90s long form** → Hook 1 (wide establishing).
- **Reels / Shorts / shorter attention** → Hook 2 (top-down cold open) or Hook 3 (detail cutaway).
- **YouTube / Vimeo / "indie short film" framing** → Hook 4 (black-to-fade).
- **24-scene tight variant** → Hook 5 (mid-conversation cold open).

The source-project winner used Hook 1; the other four are inferred patterns worth testing for future variants. Hook 1 is the safest pick because it lets the lofi music bed enter naturally and gives the viewer time to settle into the format's slow rhythm before the first dialog beat.
