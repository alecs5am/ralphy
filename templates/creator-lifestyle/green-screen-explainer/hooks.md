# Hooks — green-screen-explainer

10-12 opening lines for the 0-2s "look at this" beat. Pick by backdrop type and reaction tone. Each hook lists the backdrop type it lands on, the gesture cue the creator should hit, and the tone it locks in.

The hook contract: by the end of second 2, the viewer must know **what they're about to read** (the backdrop) and **how the creator feels about it** (face + tone). Anything that delays either side past 2s loses scroll.

---

## 1. "Look at this."

The default. Works for everything.

- **Backdrop fit:** any — screenshot, news, Reddit, chart, meme.
- **Gesture cue:** open-hand point at backdrop, eyes flick from camera to backdrop and back.
- **Tone:** neutral — leaves room for whatever follows. Use when the backdrop itself is the bombshell.

---

## 2. "I cannot believe what I just read."

Outrage primer. Sets emotional stakes before the viewer reads.

- **Backdrop fit:** Reddit post (relationship advice / AITA), news article, screenshot of someone's bad take.
- **Gesture cue:** head shake, hand to forehead OR pointing with full arm.
- **Tone:** outraged. Commits hard — don't follow with a calm-explain.

---

## 3. "Wait, what?"

Curious / sarcastic primer. Cheaper than full outrage, hooks via confusion.

- **Backdrop fit:** chart that contradicts a popular take, weird headline, contradiction screenshot.
- **Gesture cue:** double-take — look at backdrop, look at camera, look back at backdrop.
- **Tone:** curious or sarcastic. Sets up "let me explain this absurdity."

---

## 4. "This is from [source] today."

Source-credibility opener. Used when the source name itself is the news (Bloomberg / SEC / Apple keynote).

- **Backdrop fit:** news article with masthead visible, official document, press release, official chart.
- **Gesture cue:** point specifically at the masthead / source name first, then at the headline.
- **Tone:** calm-explain. Authoritative. Avoids outrage to keep credibility.

---

## 5. "Did you see what [person] just said?"

Person-react opener. The "person" must be a public figure with a real, supplied screenshot — never AI-fabricated.

- **Backdrop fit:** tweet / post / interview screenshot from the person, with handle and timestamp visible.
- **Gesture cue:** point at the person's handle / name, then at the quote.
- **Tone:** outraged or sarcastic. Match Drew Afualo energy.

---

## 6. "Take a look at this and tell me…"

Engagement-bait opener. Invites the viewer into the read.

- **Backdrop fit:** chart, ambiguous screenshot, controversial post.
- **Gesture cue:** open palm presenting the backdrop, eyes on camera.
- **Tone:** curious. Comments-fishing — works on text formats where viewers want to argue in the replies.

---

## 7. "OK so [topic] just got weird."

Casual-niche opener. Reads as "you're already in this conversation with me."

- **Backdrop fit:** tech announcement, finance chart move, sports headline.
- **Gesture cue:** small shrug + point.
- **Tone:** amused or curious. Insider energy — works for follower-base, not new viewers.

---

## 8. "Stop. Read this with me."

Slow-down opener. Forces the viewer out of scroll-mode.

- **Backdrop fit:** dense screenshot (long DM, long Reddit post, fine-print document).
- **Gesture cue:** stop hand at camera, then index finger to backdrop.
- **Tone:** calm-explain. Pairs with a longer 25-45s read where the viewer is meant to follow line-by-line.

---

## 9. "I'm not even kidding, look."

Disbelief opener. Stronger than "wait, what?" — commits to "this is real."

- **Backdrop fit:** absurd headline, screenshot of an unbelievable take, weird product launch.
- **Gesture cue:** wide eyes + emphatic point.
- **Tone:** outraged or sarcastic.

---

## 10. "[Number / stat] — and here's why that's [adjective]."

Stat-led opener for finance / data takes. The number does the hook work.

- **Backdrop fit:** chart with the headline stat highlighted, news headline with a number in it.
- **Gesture cue:** point directly at the number on the backdrop.
- **Tone:** calm-explain. Authoritative. Best for finance niche where stats sell.

---

## 11. "They actually said this. On the record."

Record-quote opener. Lower-stakes outrage, plays on disbelief that someone said it publicly.

- **Backdrop fit:** interview transcript, official statement, podcast quote screenshot with attribution.
- **Gesture cue:** point at the attribution, then at the quote.
- **Tone:** sarcastic. Don't go full outrage — let the quote do the work.

---

## 12. "So this is what we're doing now."

Cultural-moment opener. Frames the backdrop as a symptom of a larger trend.

- **Backdrop fit:** meme image, viral screenshot, cultural artifact (TikTok comment, brand ad, etc.).
- **Gesture cue:** small head-tilt, palm-up presenting.
- **Tone:** sarcastic or amused. Establishes the creator as someone "above" the trend.

If you can't pick: default to **Look at this** + the strongest tone the script supports. The hook is a frame, not the joke — the joke lives at second 5-10.

## Hook quality gate

Before render, check:

1. By second 2, is the backdrop visible and readable?
2. Is the creator's gesture pointing at the backdrop (not at camera)?
3. Does the creator's face match the tone the VO is about to deliver?
4. Are captions positioned ABOVE the creator (not over the backdrop)?

If any answer is no, regen the keyframe — fixing it later in the talking-head clip is harder.
