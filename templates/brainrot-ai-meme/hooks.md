# Brainrot AI Meme — hook library

Twelve opens that have proven repeatable in the brainrot pattern. Every hook lands in the **first 2 seconds** with VO already playing and a SCREAMING caption on screen — the gameplay loop is already running underneath. The job of these openers is exactly one thing: **stop the scroll**.

Pick one, customize the niche-specific noun, ship.

---

## 1. The "Did you know" trapdoor

**Setup.** Frame the video as a trivia drop, then immediately tease that one item is shocking enough to justify watching to the end.

**VO line.** "Did you know about these five medieval punishments — you won't believe number three."

**Audio cue.** No music. Subway Surfers SFX (jump, coin) sits underneath. VO is dry and confident.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Open loop ("number three") + concrete promise ("five things"). The brain commits to checking just to close the loop.

**Example niche.** History facts, "five weird laws still on the books", "five Roman emperors who died ridiculous deaths".

---

## 2. POV opener

**Setup.** Drop the viewer directly into the scenario as if it just happened to them.

**VO line.** "POV: you just learned that the average billionaire has fewer friends than you."

**Audio cue.** Minecraft Parkour clean wood-block SFX. VO conspiratorial-low.

**Why it stops the scroll.** First-person framing forces self-projection — the viewer is now in the scene, not watching one. POV is the most-saved format on TikTok per Bee-Social 2026.

**Example niche.** Psychology, finance trivia, dating, lifestyle.

---

## 3. The end-tease

**Setup.** Promise a payoff at a specific later timestamp. Then over-deliver at the end.

**VO line.** "Wait until you hear what this guy did at the very end of his trial — it's insane."

**Audio cue.** No music. Gameplay SFX only. VO is dramatic-narrator.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Pure curiosity gap. The viewer rationalizes the watch by "I'll just see how it ends." Watch-completion = ranking signal.

**Example niche.** True crime, history, conspiracy.

---

## 4. The TRUE STORY callout

**Setup.** Lead with a wild claim, immediately label it "TRUE STORY" in screaming text.

**VO line.** "This man married his own mother by accident — TRUE STORY."

**Audio cue.** GTA chase music low (diegetic from gameplay). VO sarcastic.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Wild claim + authority hedge ("TRUE STORY") creates the "wait, really?" moment. Comments fill with "no way bro".

**Example niche.** History, true crime, weird news, conspiracy.

---

## 5. The Top-N listicle

**Setup.** Numbered countdown of N things, escalating in shock value to N=1.

**VO line.** "Top five facts about your brain that will actually make you uncomfortable. Number five —"

**Audio cue.** Subway Surfers coins on each list-item beat. VO confident.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Built-in pacing structure means the brain knows what it's getting. Each item is a mini-loop closing inside the bigger loop. Listicles are the most-templatizable format per the comparative table.

**Example niche.** Psychology, finance, fitness, productivity, edutainment.

---

## 6. The "this is illegal in" trapdoor

**Setup.** Frame an everyday behavior as illegal somewhere bizarre.

**VO line.** "This is illegal in seventeen countries — and you probably do it every morning."

**Audio cue.** Minecraft Parkour. VO conspiratorial.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Specificity ("seventeen") + accusation ("you probably do it") = self-implication hook. Viewer must check whether they're guilty.

**Example niche.** Travel oddities, law trivia, lifestyle.

---

## 7. The contradicted-expert hook

**Setup.** Open with an authoritative-sounding claim, then immediately undermine it.

**VO line.** "Doctors told you to drink eight glasses a day. They were wrong — and the truth is worse."

**Audio cue.** Subway Surfers. VO dramatic narrator.

**Why it stops the scroll.** "They were wrong" is a betrayal frame. The brain recalibrates and listens for the new authority claim.

**Example niche.** Health myths, finance myths, productivity myths, history myths.

---

## 8. The numerical shock

**Setup.** Lead with a specific, surprisingly-large number.

**VO line.** "Three thousand eight hundred and twelve people disappear in US national parks every year. Here's why the government doesn't talk about it."

**Audio cue.** No music. Gameplay SFX only. VO whisper.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Big specific number = legibility + curiosity. "Why doesn't the government talk about it" = conspiracy-frame Pavlov.

**Example niche.** Conspiracy, true crime, weird statistics.

---

## 9. The "you've been doing X wrong" hook

**Setup.** Accuse the viewer of a tiny, universal mistake.

**VO line.** "You've been brushing your teeth wrong your entire life — and your dentist won't tell you why."

**Audio cue.** Minecraft Parkour. VO matter-of-fact.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Direct accusation + authority withhold. Viewer feels seen and slightly defensive — they'll watch to refute.

**Example niche.** Lifestyle hacks, productivity, fitness, psychology.

---

## 10. The cold-open question

**Setup.** Pose a question with no setup, then answer with a hook of its own.

**VO line.** "Why do all airline pilots wear watches? It's not for the time."

**Audio cue.** Subway Surfers. VO dramatic.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Question + immediate denial of the obvious answer = the brain is now compelled to find the real one.

**Example niche.** Trivia, history, psychology, edutainment-disguised.

---

## 11. The "this man" character intro

**Setup.** Introduce an unnamed protagonist with one wild attribute.

**VO line.** "This man owns more koalas than any government on earth — and he's not happy about it."

**Audio cue.** GTA. VO sarcastic.

**Why it stops the scroll.** Anonymity ("this man") forces the viewer to keep watching to learn who. The "not happy about it" twist is a pre-loaded curiosity gap.

**Example niche.** Weird-news, history, eccentric biographies.

---

## 12. The math-impossible hook

**Setup.** State a comparison that sounds mathematically wrong.

**VO line.** "There are more bacteria in your mouth right now than people who have ever lived on earth."

**Audio cue.** No music. VO awed.

**Why it stops the scroll.** The brain pauses to verify the comparison. That pause is the entire battle won.

**Example niche.** Science facts, psychology, biology, "scale" content.
