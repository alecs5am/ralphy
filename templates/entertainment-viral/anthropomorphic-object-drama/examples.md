# Worked examples — Anthropomorphic Object Drama

Two complete variants showing how the same 7-beat structure adapts to different object classes + plot tropes. Source project (`fruit-drama-001`) was Variant 0 — fruit cast, affair plot. The two below are how a future agent would adapt the template.

---

## Variant 1 — Vegetable Drama: "The Carrot Couple's Crisis"

**Plot trope:** rivalry / professional jealousy → reconciliation
**Object class:** root vegetables + leafy greens
**Target language:** English
**Duration:** 7 × 8s = 56s

### Cast (lock-strings)

- **character_a — `chef-carrot-husband`**: "a Pixar-3D cartoon orange carrot-headed man, smooth tapered bright orange carrot head with three tall green leaves on top, kind brown eyes, small confident smile, wearing a slightly stained white chef jacket with rolled sleeves, blue chef pants, mid-30s passionate-cook vibe"
- **character_b — `kale-wife`**: "a Pixar-3D cartoon kale-headed woman, glossy curly dark-green kale-leaf head with delicate purple veining, bright hazel eyes, soft expression, wearing a pastel lavender wrap dress, gentle nurturing posture"
- **character_c — `truffle-rival`**: "a Pixar-3D cartoon woman with a single big glossy dark-brown truffle as her head, gold flecks across the surface, mysterious half-lidded gold eyes, glossy deep-burgundy lips, wearing a fitted black cocktail dress, single gold earring, knowing smirk"
- **character_d — `bell-pepper-newguy`**: "a Pixar-3D cartoon yellow bell-pepper-headed man, smooth glossy yellow pepper head with three rounded lobes, chiseled jawline, warm green eyes behind clear glasses, brown corduroy blazer over a cream t-shirt, late-30s gentle-intellectual vibe"

### Beat sheet

| Scene | Beat | Speaker | Line (EN) | Caption pop |

| 01 | Hook — chef husband leaving for work | kale-wife, chef-carrot | "Don't burn yourself today, my fiery carrot." / "Save dinner for me, my leafy queen." | FAMILY |
| 02 | Setup — truffle rival in the restaurant kitchen | truffle-rival | "Stay late tonight, chef. There's something only YOU can taste." | STAY |
| 03 | Rising — tasting forbidden truffle | truffle-rival | "Sweeter than that bitter little kale of yours." | SWEET |
| 04 | Discovery — gold flecks on his cheek | kale-wife | "What's that gold on your face? That's not my seasoning." | GOLD |
| 05 | Confrontation — restaurant review email | kale-wife (SHOUT) | "Ten years of meal prep, carrot! Ten years — for a fungus in a black dress?!" | TEN YEARS |
| 06 | Departure — packing the herb garden | chef-carrot | "Kale, wait! Come back, my heart is wilting!" | WILTING |
| 07 | Resolution — farmers market new chapter | bell-pepper-newguy | "Hey there, kale. I'm Pepper. You're way too leafy to be alone." | PEPPER |

Warm acoustic guitar + light strings opening (morning kitchen warmth), growing tension with quiet timpani at 16s, dramatic swelling strings + sharp staccato at the betrayal beat at 32s, then warm hopeful piano resolution at 48s. Pixar emotional storytelling vibe."

## How to use these examples

1. Pick an object class and plot trope. Both variants above keep the SAME 7-beat structure as the source (`fruit-drama-001`); only cast + setting + dialogue change.
2. Write your 4 character lock-strings (≥80 words each). Use the variants' lock-strings as length / depth reference.
3. Adapt the 7 lines to your cast's term-of-endearment. The phrase pattern "my [adjective] [object_class]" is load-bearing in lines 01, 03, 05, 06 — keep it.
4. Mirror the music brief literally; just swap the genre / instrumentation if a different mood fits.
5. Generate scene-01 keyframe FIRST as a smoke-test. If the face-on-object cast-lineup reads correctly there, proceed with the other 6. If not, iterate on lock-strings before burning $4 on Veo.
