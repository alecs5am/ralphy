# Hooks — 0–2s patterns

The 0–2s hook is **scene-01 first half**: a face-on-object cold-open + the first dialogue line. The trick is that the visual reveal (face-on-fruit / face-on-appliance / face-on-vegetable) is enough to pattern-interrupt scrolling on its own. The dialogue line then commits to the dead-serious telenovela register so the viewer locks in.

## Anatomy of the source hook (scene-01, fruit-drama-001)

- **0.0–0.1s** — opening frame: warm pastel kitchen, two face-on-fruit characters at the door
- **0.1–3.5s** — character_b (strawberry-wife) speaks first ON CAMERA: *"Have a great day at work, my sweet banana."*
- **3.5–8.0s** — character_a (banana-husband) responds + kisses on cheek + wave: *"I'll be home for dinner, my little strawberry."*
- **Popping-word caption** at the strongest word — source used baked `СЕМЬЯ` (family) as the on-screen pop

The hook is the cast-lineup reveal + first dialogue beat fused into one 8s clip. The reveal "wait, that fruit is a person who's married to that other fruit" is the scroll-stopper; the line "Have a great day at work, my sweet banana" carries the telenovela register.

## Hook pattern (template)

```
{{character_a_lock_string}} faces {{character_b_lock_string}} in a {{warm everyday setting — kitchen / front door / bedroom}}.
{{character_b}} speaks first ON CAMERA, {{character_b_voice}} in {{target_language}}: "{{everyday-warmth line that names the partner with a {{subject_object_class}} term of endearment}}".
{{character_b}} {{small affectionate gesture — kiss / wave / hand-touch}}.
{{character_a}} responds ON CAMERA, {{character_a_voice}} in {{target_language}}: "{{everyday-warmth reply naming the partner with a {{subject_object_class}} term of endearment}}".
```

## Three concrete hook variants

### Variant A — "perfect family morning" (the source pattern)
- Setting: warm pastel kitchen, morning sun, family photo on the wall
- character_b line: *"Have a great day at work, my sweet {{character_a_id_term}}."*
- character_a line: *"I'll be home for dinner, my little {{character_b_id_term}}."*
- Term of endearment: literal `{{subject_object_class}}` name (banana / strawberry / etc.)
- Why it works: everyday warmth + face-on-object reveal = maximum dissonance for the audience

### Variant B — "perfect couple goodbye at the door"
- Setting: front porch, suitcase by his feet, sunny garden behind
- character_b line: *"Hurry home, my crisp {{character_a_id_term}}. I miss you already."*
- character_a line: *"You're the sweetest {{character_b_id_term}} a {{character_a_id_term}} ever had."*
- Use when the plot trope is a business-trip-affair (departure framing reframes the hook setup)

### Variant C — "perfect partner in bed Sunday morning"
- Setting: rumpled bed, pastel sheets, sun through curtains
- character_b line: *"Don't get up yet, my warm {{character_a_id_term}}."*
- character_a line: *"Whatever you say, my soft {{character_b_id_term}}."*
- Use when the plot trope leans intimate-betrayal (intimate hook makes the betrayal in scene-02/03 hit harder)

## Anti-patterns for hooks

- **DO NOT** open on the antagonist (character_c). The hook needs to establish the "perfect" baseline first; the antagonist is the setup beat (scene-02). Opening on the antagonist breaks the dramatic-irony reveal.
- **DO NOT** open with a narrator hook ("Watch what happens when…"). The format is on-camera-dialogue-only. Narrator framing kills the telenovela register immediately.
- **DO NOT** open with text-on-screen. The popping-word caption fires later, on the emotional peak. Text-on-screen at 0s competes with the face-on-object reveal.
- **DO NOT** open on a wide / establishing shot of the location. Open on the two leads in a medium two-shot — the reveal IS the face-on-object pair.
- **DO NOT** rush the dialogue. The full 8s should land both lines comfortably; if there's only time for one line, the second beat (scene-02) is too compressed.

## How the hook composes with the music

The music bed starts at sec 0 with the warm pastel piano + pizzicato — it must already be warm/sweet before any dialogue lands. The first dialogue line lands on top of the warm bed at ~0.1s. This is critical: a cold-cut music start ruins the hook. The bed should run -22 LUFS under VO so the dialogue stays the focal point.
