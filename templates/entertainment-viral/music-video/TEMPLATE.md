# Music Video — Template

A beat-synced visual piece where the **song is the spine** and every cut, camera move, color shift, or particle burst is anchored to a specific musical event (kick, snare, hi-hat, vocal line, harmonic change, drop). Works for short-form vertical (TikTok/Reels/Shorts, 15–30s) and long-form horizontal (YouTube, 60s+).

This is a **vibe-style** template — it does not prescribe a single scenario. It prescribes the *grammar* of music video: the rules that separate a music video from "a video with music laid on top".

---

## Why this template exists

99% of "music videos" generated by AI tools are slideshows with a song on top. Cuts land on arbitrary frames. Energy doesn't track the song. The drop arrives and nothing visual happens. This template encodes the producer-side discipline that fixes that:

1. **Extract the beat grid first, edit second.** Real BPM from the track (or a user-stated target) drives cut placement. No vibe edits.
2. **Match cut density to song density.** Verse → 1 cut per 2 bars. Chorus → 1 per bar. Drop → 1 per beat. Hi-hat sections can go to 1 per half-beat.
3. **Energy arc mirrors song structure.** Verse low, build climbs, chorus peaks, bridge resets, final chorus exceeds, outro resolves.
4. **Genre dictates the visual language.** Hip-hop is not pop is not lo-fi. The cookbook has a per-genre palette + lighting + hook recipe.

---

## Vibe anchors (the non-negotiables)

These are what make a music video read as a music video instead of stock footage on a soundtrack. If you cut any of these, you're outside the template.

### 1. Beat-cut sync
Every cut lands on a musical event — kick, snare, vocal phrase start, harmonic change. Cuts that land on silence or between beats are a defect, not a stylistic choice. The composition stage uses the extracted beat grid (or BPM-derived synthetic grid) to align clip boundaries to the nearest beat.

### 2. Energy arc that tracks the song
You can map any music video to a curve: low → climb → peak → reset → higher peak → resolve. The visual must follow that curve. A chorus visual that's calmer than the verse is broken. A drop without a peak hit is broken.

### 3. Performance vs. Narrative split (declared up front)
Three modes, pick one and commit:
- **Performance** — Artist is the subject. Camera is on them. Their movement, expression, and styling carry the visual.
- **Narrative** — A story unfolds alongside the song. The artist may or may not appear. Cuts to story beats.
- **Abstract / Visualizer** — No performer. Pure motion, particles, geometry, color responding to audio. Best for instrumental or experimental tracks.

Mixed-mode is allowed (e.g. performance verse → abstract drop) but must be declared.

### 4. Genre-honest visual language
You don't shoot hip-hop with pastel candy lighting. You don't shoot pop with deep noir shadows. The genre-mood matrix in `prompt-cookbook.md` gives the canonical palette + lighting + hook patterns per genre. Deviation is allowed but must be intentional and stated.

### 5. Hook in the first 2 seconds
Short-form (TikTok/Reels) demands a visual hook before the algorithm scrolls past. The hook lands at or before t=2s and aligns with the first significant musical event. See `hooks.md` for 8–12 patterns.

## Variation axes

What you change per project to keep the template generative:

| Axis | Range |
|---|---|
| Strategy mode | performance / narrative / abstract-visualizer / mixed |
| Genre | hip-hop, pop, rock, EDM, R&B, lo-fi, jazz, classical, country, K-pop |
| Color palette | genre-dictated; see cookbook matrix |
| Lighting context | concert (haze, lasers, strobes) / studio (controlled, hero) / location (natural, environmental) / abstract-digital (neon, particles) |
| Performer count | solo / duo / band / dance crew / no-performer |
| Cut rate | slow-cinematic (≤80 BPM feel) / mid-pop (90–120 BPM) / rapid-edm (128–160 BPM) |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical (short-form) / 16:9 horizontal (YouTube) / 1:1 (album-loop) |
| Duration | 15s teaser, 30s short-form, 60s loop, full-track 2–4min |

## Narrative arc (per song-structure section)

Default skeleton. Adjust to actual track structure.

| Section | Duration share | Energy | Visual brief |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intro / cold open | 2–4s | low to building | The hook. One striking image. Establishes genre + palette. Usually a single shot. |
| Verse 1 | 15–25% | low–mid | Establishes performer / world. 1 cut per 2 bars. Camera slow. Lighting motivated, not theatrical. |
| Pre-chorus / build | 5–10% | climbing | Cut rate doubles. Camera starts moving. Color saturation creeps up. Tension. |
| Chorus 1 | 15–20% | peak | Visual hook lands. 1 cut per bar minimum. Full palette. Hero lighting. The thing the listener will remember. |
| Verse 2 | 10–15% | mid (not full reset) | New angle, new costume, new location — same world. Don't redo verse 1. |
| Bridge / breakdown | 8–12% | resets, then re-builds | Visual surprise: new color, new location, broken rhythm, slow-mo, or full silence. |
| Final chorus / drop | 15–25% | exceeds chorus 1 | Everything chorus 1 had, plus: more performers, more particles, faster cuts, higher contrast, or new layer. |
| Outro | 3–8s | resolution or hard stop | Either fade to a still image (resolution) or one last impact frame (hard stop). |

## Inputs (what the user must / can supply)

### Required
- **Genre or mood reference** — at minimum, a genre name. Better: 1–2 reference music videos (URLs) to match vibe.
- **Strategy mode declaration** — performance / narrative / abstract-visualizer.

### Strongly recommended
- **The track itself** (mp3/wav, uploaded as reference). Without it, the editor uses a synthetic beat grid at user-stated BPM, which is workable for visualizers but weak for narrative/performance.
- **BPM target** — if no track, state BPM. Drives cut rate.
- **Duration target** — short-form (15–30s), loop (60s), full-track (2–4min).

### Conditional (reference-required gate)
- **Performer reference photo** — REQUIRED if the video features a named real person (artist, influencer). Without it, the gate refuses generation. AI-improvised faces of real people are never acceptable.
- **Location / mood-board references** — optional, improves cross-scene consistency.

### Optional
- **Lyrics** — unlocks lyric-video variant with KaraokeCaptions.
- **No track at all** — ElevenLabs Music composes one from a genre + mood + duration brief. State the brief; the producer will spec the music call.

## When this template is the wrong choice

- **The user wants a product ad with a song on it.** That's `before-after-product` or `product-demo`, not music video. The song serves the product, not vice versa.
- **The user wants a podcast clip / talking-head with bg music.** That's `talking-head`, not music video. The voice is the spine, not the song.
- **The user has no genre, no track, no BPM, and no vibe reference.** Refuse. Music video without a musical anchor is just montage. Ask one clarifying question that gets you any one of those four anchors.
- **Named-artist deepfake.** If the user wants "make a music video with [Real Famous Artist]" without supplying licensed reference + consent, refuse on the reference-required gate. No exceptions.

---

## Quality gates (refuse, don't warn)

A music video is **rejected** at the scenario stage if:

1. No beat anchor declared (no track, no BPM, no genre BPM range).
2. Cut points in the scenario don't reference musical events (kick, snare, vocal start, drop, harmonic change).
3. Energy arc is flat — chorus visual brief is the same intensity as verse.
4. Genre is declared but palette / lighting in the prompts contradicts the genre-mood matrix without a stated reason.
5. Strategy mode (performance / narrative / abstract) was not declared, so prompts are mixing performer focus with abstract visualizer in the same scene without intent.

Two gate failures in a row → stop and report concrete options. Do not render mp4.
