# Hooks — Food & Beverage

The first 2 seconds decide whether the viewer keeps watching. For food, the hook is always a **sensory event** — a single piece of texture / heat / motion that triggers appetite before the brain has time to evaluate "is this an ad?"

Pick ONE hook per video. Mixing two reads as confused. Each hook below names: the action, the angle, the lighting cue, and the dominant ASMR sound to layer.

## 1. Cheese Pull / Stretch

**Action.** Top of burger / slice / grilled cheese being lifted, melted cheese stretching in a single strand.
**Angle.** Side, 45° to the food, eye-level with the patty/slice. Strand is in profile so the stretch is fully visible.
**Lighting.** Backlight on the strand specifically — makes melted cheese *glow*. Warm key 45° from camera-left.
**Sound.** Soft sizzle (faint, persistent) + the subtle *tear* the moment the strand breaks. Slow-mo the break.
**Why it works.** The stretch is a literal physics demo of "this is melted, hot, and real." Viewers pre-cognitively read it as quality.

## 2. Slow-Motion Sauce / Glaze Pour

**Action.** Viscous sauce (béarnaise, chocolate, caramel, hot honey, pesto) cascading from spoon or jug onto plated food.
**Angle.** 45° low, looking up at the pour. Backlight is essential.
**Lighting.** Backlight makes the falling liquid translucent and glowing. Warm fill from front to keep the food readable.
**Sound.** Single dominant pour sound — thick, slow, slightly viscous. No music spike during the pour itself.
**Why it works.** The pour is an irreversible, abundant, generous moment. It signals "this dish is loaded."

## 3. Steam Reveal Under Lid / From Bowl

**Action.** Lid lifting off a hot dish (ramen, dim sum, soup) or steam pluming from a just-plated bowl. The reveal is the steam, not the food.
**Angle.** 45° side or directly behind the steam plume so it backlights into camera.
**Lighting.** Hard backlight (warm tungsten or golden window light) — without backlight, steam disappears on i2v output.
**Sound.** Soft hiss of escaping vapor + faint bubble / boil underneath. Almost-silence works here too.
**Why it works.** Steam = "this just left the kitchen." Visceral freshness signal.

## 4. Sizzle on Hot Pan

**Action.** Protein, vegetable, or noodle hitting a screaming-hot pan. Oil jumps, color changes from raw to seared in real time.
**Angle.** Overhead or low 30° looking across the pan surface. Both work — overhead reads as recipe, low reads as restaurant.
**Lighting.** Bright top key + backlight on the rising steam. Bounced fill on the pan's matte surface.
**Sound.** Sizzle is the whole sound. Loud, sustained, layered — the more the better here. ASMR territory.
**Why it works.** Sizzle is the sound of caramelization. Browned food is, biologically, what humans evolved to want.

## 5. Knife Cut Through Yielding Crust

**Action.** Blade slicing through fried chicken, schnitzel, croquette, crispy salmon skin — crispy exterior shatters, soft interior reveals.
**Angle.** Macro side angle locked on the cut surface. The blade enters from frame-right and the camera doesn't move.
**Lighting.** Side light (raking) to make the crust texture jump. Slight backlight on the steam from the interior.
**Sound.** The crunch of the crust + the wet slip of the interior. Two-part sound. Slow-mo doubles its impact.
**Why it works.** Texture contrast (crispy / creamy) is the most under-used cue in food video. Viewers read it as craft.

## 6. Bread / Pastry Tear with Steam Release

**Action.** Hands tearing fresh bread, croissant, focaccia, dinner roll. Interior crumb opens, steam puffs out.
**Angle.** Eye-level with the hands, 45°, fairly tight. Hands stay in frame — they signal warmth and intimacy.
**Lighting.** Warm window light from camera-left + soft backlight catching the steam wisps.
**Sound.** Soft fibrous tear (not a snap — it's bread, not a chip) + the breathy release of steam.
**Why it works.** The tear feels human, immediate, like the camera is in the kitchen. Inverts the polished-ad register.

## 7. Latte Art Pour Completion

**Action.** Final 2 seconds of milk pour into espresso — the rosetta or heart resolving into focus as the pitcher pulls away.
**Angle.** Tight overhead or 45° looking down into the cup. The cup nearly fills the frame.
**Lighting.** Soft warm window light. The brown / white contrast is the visual.
**Sound.** The quiet pour + a single soft *tap* as the pitcher lifts. Sometimes café ambience underneath.
**Why it works.** Skill on display. Viewers read latte art as "this place cares about coffee."

## 8. Spirit / Cocktail Pour Over Ice

**Action.** Whiskey, mezcal, or amaro pouring from bottle into rocks glass with one large clear ice cube.
**Angle.** Tight on the glass, side angle, slight push-in. The bottle enters from above.
**Lighting.** Moody bar lighting — low ambient + bottle backlight catching the spirit color (amber, ruby, clear gold).
**Sound.** Glug of spirit + the crack/clink of ice settling under the new liquid. Faint jazz or vinyl noise underneath.
**Why it works.** The amber translucency + ice shift is a high-craft visual that reads as adult / luxe / ritual.

## 9. Fizz / Bubble Eruption

**Action.** Soda, sparkling water, beer head, or champagne — bubbles rising en masse, head forming, foam climbing.
**Angle.** Side macro on the glass at the liquid line. The bubbles fill 70% of the frame.
**Lighting.** Hard backlight through the liquid — bubbles become tiny lights. Warm or cool depending on drink.
**Sound.** Pure fizz, sustained. Maybe a soft pop at the start (cap / cork). No music spike.
**Why it works.** Effervescence reads as refreshment, energy, celebration. Beats still drinks every time.

## 10. Ingredient Cascade / Garnish Rain

**Action.** Salt, herbs, chili flakes, sesame seeds, grated cheese — sprinkled from above onto the finished dish in slow-mo.
**Angle.** Eye-level on the dish, hand entering from top of frame. Or overhead with the seasoning falling away from camera.
**Lighting.** Backlight to make every falling particle visible. Warm key on the dish.
**Sound.** Soft scatter sound — almost silence, with a small sprinkle whisper. Lets the visual do the work.
**Why it works.** The fall implies "fresh, last-second, made-to-order." Particles in motion = production value.

## 11. Chocolate / Caramel Snap

**Action.** Thick chocolate bar broken in half, caramel shard cracked, brittle snapped — interior reveals, shards fall.
**Angle.** Tight side, hands on either edge. Slow-mo the break.
**Lighting.** Side light to catch the matte / glossy contrast on the broken edge. Warm.
**Sound.** Sharp clean snap. Single sound. No layering needed.
**Why it works.** The break is a quality test — only well-tempered chocolate / properly-cooked sugar snaps cleanly.

## 12. First-Bite Cross-Section

**Action.** Hands lifting burger / sandwich / wrap to mouth, first bite taken, cross-section revealed as it pulls away.
**Angle.** Eye-level, tight on the food + lower face / hands. Bite happens at center frame.
**Lighting.** Bright bounced fill — needs to read clearly, no moodiness. Slight backlight on the food's edge.
**Sound.** The bite itself: crunch / squish / cheese pull combined. The single most loaded ASMR moment in food video.
**Why it works.** The cross-section reveals everything the viewer could not see from outside. Layers = craft. Bite = permission.

**Choose by category, fast:**

- Coffee / café → #7 (latte art) or #6 (pastry tear)
- Burger / fast food → #1 (cheese pull) or #12 (first bite)
- Cocktail / bar → #8 (spirit pour) or #9 (fizz)
- Asian / ramen / wok → #3 (steam reveal) or #4 (sizzle)
- Pasta / italian → #1 (mozzarella stretch), #2 (sauce pour), or #10 (cheese cascade)
- BBQ / grill → #4 (sizzle) or #5 (knife cut)
- Dessert / pastry → #11 (chocolate snap), #2 (caramel pour), or #6 (croissant tear)
- Salad / fresh / smoothie → #10 (ingredient cascade) or #9 (fizz, for drinks)
- Street food → #12 (first bite) or #4 (sizzle)
