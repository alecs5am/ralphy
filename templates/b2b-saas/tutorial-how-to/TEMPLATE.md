# Tutorial / How-To — vibe style

**Genre:** search-first 3-step tutorial / how-to (Hoyos method).
**Length:** 45-60s. 60s beats 15s by ~3× per Inflow Network on Shorts.
**Format:** YouTube Shorts (primary, search-first king), TikTok, Instagram Reels — 9:16, 30fps, 1080×1920.

> **Reference is optional**, but if the brief names a real product, app, branded ingredient, or person, follow AGENTS.md hard rule #3 and require a reference. AI will hallucinate logos and UI every time.

## Why this works

The tutorial format is the only short-form pattern that earns search traffic for months after publish. While trend-driven formats peak in 3-7 days and decay, "how to X" content compounds.

1. **Search-first.** 37% of consumers (41% of Gen Z) start product / how-to searches inside social rather than Google (Sprout Social Q2 2025). YouTube's algorithm aggressively surfaces evergreen tutorial Shorts.
2. **Open loop.** The hook promises a payoff. The viewer must reach step 3 to collect it. Retention is structural, not personality-driven.
3. **Completion rate beats reach.** Jenny Hoyos (1.69M subs, 954M+ views, frequent 10M+ Shorts at ~34s) targets a 90% average view duration. The algorithm rewards completion over thumbs.
4. **Length inverts intuition.** On Shorts, 60s outperforms 15s by ~3× (Inflow Network). The format breathes; you're not racing the clock.
5. **Niche-agnostic.** Same skeleton works for cooking, productivity, AI tools, finance, and beauty hacks. The hook word and the payoff carry the niche flavor.

## Vibe anchors

- **Power-word hook in the first 1.5 seconds.** Free / Banned / Secret / $1 / Stolen / Illegal-feeling / Nobody / Wrong. The word is the first thing said and the first thing on screen. See `hooks.md` for the 12 patterns.
- **Foreshadow at 2-5s, never spoil the steps.** Show the final state ("here's what you'll get") without telling how. This sets the open loop. Example: "I made a $90 dinner for $4 — here's how."
- **Three steps, three named transitions.** Not "step 1, step 2, step 3" — that's robotic. Use Hoyos-style transitions: "Let's get cooking" / "Now the magic" / "Here's the trick" / "And finally" / "Last move". Each transition is a 0.5s wipe with a step counter (1/3, 2/3, 3/3).
- **Emotional payoff at the end.** Not just "and that's it" — a visible reaction, a first bite, a number on screen, a before-after side-by-side. The payoff is what you promised in the foreshadow.
- **Tight cadence.** No dead air, no "um," no setup that isn't the foreshadow. Hoyos cuts ~every 1-2 seconds inside steps.
- **Big step counters on screen.** 1/3, 2/3, 3/3 in a corner the entire step, not just at the transition. Visible progress is what makes 60s feel like 30s.
- **Burned-in captions.** 85% of mobile views are muted. Captions are the script.

## Narrative arc

```
0-2s    → Power-word hook. Word lands within 1.5s.
          On-screen text matches the spoken word. Eye contact on camera, or hands-on-the-thing.
          VO: "Free X." / "Banned in 3 states." / "I made $90 worth of food for $4."

2-5s    → Foreshadow. Show the final result for ~1s.
          Tease, do not explain. The viewer now has the open loop.
          VO: "Here's how." / "Watch this." / "Three steps."

5-20s   → Step 1. Named transition wipe ("Let's get cooking" / "First move").
          Counter 1/3 visible the entire beat.
          Single concrete action, 1-2s cuts inside.
          VO: explain WHILE doing, not before.

20-35s  → Step 2. Named transition ("Now the magic" / "Here's the trick").
          Counter 2/3. Same pace.

35-50s  → Step 3. Named transition ("And finally" / "Last move").
          Counter 3/3. Slightly tighter cuts to build anticipation.

50-60s  → Payoff. Slow down 15%. Hold the result on screen 1.5-2s.
          Visible reaction (taste, click, reveal, number).
          VO: short, punchy. "And that's $4." / "That's it." / "From 4 hours to 4 minutes."
          Optional CTA card last 1s: "Try it. Tag me."
```

## Required user inputs

1. **Topic** — what problem this tutorial solves, in one sentence. ("How to cut your AWS bill in half.")
2. **Three specific steps** — concrete, demonstrable actions. Not abstract advice. ("Open Cost Explorer" beats "audit your spending.")
3. **Hook word** — one power word from the `hooks.md` patterns, plus the one-line hook around it. Keep it ≤ 7 words.
4. **Payoff line** — what the viewer walks away seeing / feeling / saving. Concrete. ("$4 dinner." "10 minutes back." "Smooth under-eyes.")
5. *(Optional)* **Reference** — if the tutorial features a specific app UI, branded product, or named person, supply a reference. Otherwise skip.
6. *(Optional)* **Voice tone** — energetic-Hoyos (default) / calm-explainer / authoritative-expert.
7. *(Optional)* **VO language** — defaults to English; works in any language but search-first benefit is language-bound.

## When NOT to use

- **Topic too complex for 60s.** "How to set up Kubernetes from scratch" is not a Shorts tutorial — it's a long-form. If you can't honestly demonstrate three concrete steps in 45 seconds, switch to long-form or pick a narrower slice ("the one Kubernetes command I run every day").
- **Pure entertainment / no payoff.** If the goal is laughs or vibes rather than teaching, use `talking-head-rant`, `social-hook`, or a storytime template instead.
- **No demonstrable visual.** Pure-conceptual advice ("how to think about money") with nothing to film flattens. Either find the visual demo or pick a different format.
- **Brand storytelling.** Use `brand-story` (12-) or `before-after-product` (which already lives in this template pack) instead.
- **Length > 90s.** The retention curve falls off a cliff past 90s for tutorial Shorts. If you need more time, the format is wrong.
- **Niches that punish keyword-stuffing.** Highly entertainment-driven niches (comedy, music) don't compound from search the same way — the search-first benefit evaporates.
