# 2-Second Hook Framework

The single most important 2 seconds in any cinematic short. Mobile retention falls off a cliff at the 2s mark; films that win the open have 3-5x higher completion rates. This file is the menu — pick one (or stack 2-3) for every prompt.

## Why 2 seconds

- Human attention triggers at 0.5-2s at the unconscious level.
- TikTok / Reels / Shorts algorithms all weight retention past the 2s mark heavily.
- Great cinema opens with impact: *Blade Runner 2049* opens with a neon city glow; *Dunkirk* opens with chaos; *1917* opens with a tracking shot already in motion.

The hook is not "the establishing shot." The hook is what makes the viewer's thumb stop. The establishing shot comes after.

## The 12 hooks

### 1. Black Screen to Light Burst
**Setup:** Pure black, complete silence, 0-0.8s. At 0.8s, explosive light burst from a specified direction (top-left, dead center, frame-right). Color of burst defines mood: amber=epic, cool blue=noir, hot pink=cyberpunk.
**Why it works:** Audio-visual contrast hits the brain as a hard event. Lens flare blooming sells production value instantly.
**Phrasing:** "Begin in pure black. At 0.8s, explosive amber light burst from frame-top-left. Light floods frame in 0.3s. Lens flare blooming across center."

### 2. Extreme Macro Snap to Wide Reveal
**Setup:** 0-0.5s extreme macro of a small detail (water droplet, dust mote, fabric weave, eye iris). At 0.5s, whip-cut to extreme wide shot of the landscape. Scale shift is 100:1.
**Why it works:** Cognitive dissonance. The brain spends 1-2s recognizing what it just saw, by which point it's committed.
**Phrasing:** "Open with extreme macro close-up of water droplet refracting light. At 0.5s, whip-cut to extreme wide shot of desert valley at golden hour. Scale ratio 100:1."

### 3. Reverse Motion
**Setup:** Action runs backwards in the first 2s. Water droplets float upward, smoke swirls counterclockwise, an object slides in reverse across frame.
**Why it works:** Unnatural motion = immediately interesting. The brain flags "this is not normal" and demands attention.
**Phrasing:** "Action moves backwards in first 2 seconds: water droplets float upward, smoke swirls counterclockwise. Hypnotic, impossible motion. Resolves to forward time at 2s."

### 4. Silent Beat Then Explosive Sound
**Setup:** 0-1.2s complete silence — no music, no dialogue, near-zero ambient. At 1.3s, sudden violent sound: gunshot, music drop, glass shatter, door slam. Visual cut/freeze/blur synchronized to the audio moment.
**Why it works:** Audio contrast creates visceral physical reaction. Earbud users feel the drop.
**Phrasing:** "First 1.2s: complete silence. At 1.3s, sudden percussive kick drum hit at +3dB. Synchronized whip-cut to high-speed motion."

### 5. Extreme Color Shift
**Setup:** Frame opens cool desaturated grey-blue. At 0.6s, sudden color correction to warm amber-gold or saturated neon. 50% color shift over 0.4s.
**Why it works:** Reads as a "time jump" or "reality shift." Viewers stay to find out what changed.
**Phrasing:** "Frame opens in cool desaturated blue-grey. At 0.6s, sudden shift to warm amber-gold saturation. Creates reality-shift feel."

### 6. Fast Movement Entering Frame
**Setup:** Subject enters frame from edge (not center) at high velocity. Speeding car, sprinting figure, falling object, or camera push-in at 5+ ft/s. Leading lines guide the entry.
**Why it works:** Peripheral motion catches the eye even on a scrolling feed. Forces tracking.
**Phrasing:** "Subject enters frame from frame-right at high-speed (simulated 45 mph equivalent). Motion blur trails 20% opacity. Leading lines from frame-edge to subject position."

### 7. Rack Focus Across Two Planes
**Setup:** Foreground and background both initially sharp at f/1.4 equivalent. At 1s, focus racks dramatically — foreground tack-sharp, background dissolves into bokeh, or vice versa.
**Why it works:** Professional cinema technique signals "this is real cinema." Draws attention to the chosen plane.
**Phrasing:** "Two planes of focus, both sharp 0-1s. At 1s, focus racks to foreground. Background dissolves into circular bokeh. f/1.4 cinema-equivalent shallow depth."

### 8. Stark Geometric Contrast
**Setup:** First 2s emphasize extreme compositional geometry — sharp horizontal lines vs verticals, concentric circles vs straight edges. High-contrast lighting emphasizes the geometry. Asymmetrical frame.
**Why it works:** Visual tension without narrative tension. The brain wants to resolve the composition.
**Phrasing:** "Frame composition: hard horizontal shadow line bisecting frame, vertical light shaft cutting through. High-contrast chiaroscuro. Asymmetrical."

### 9. Eyes Snap Open / Direct Eye Contact
**Setup:** Close shot of eyes in low light. At 0.8s, eyes snap open or lock on-camera. Optional: dilating-pupil effect, subtle head tilt.
**Why it works:** The most primal form of attention capture. Human brains are evolutionarily wired to track other eyes.
**Phrasing:** "Close shot of eyes in low-light, lids closed. At 0.8s, eyes snap open and lock direct contact with camera. Pupil dilates over 0.3s."

### 10. Disorienting Camera Rotation
**Setup:** Camera tilts, rolls, or rotates rapidly in the first 1.5s. 45-180 degree rotation. Tilted horizon. Stabilizes to level frame at 2s.
**Why it works:** Slight vertigo signals "something is happening / something is wrong." Viewers stay to see what.
**Phrasing:** "Camera rotates 90 degrees clockwise over 0-1.2s. Tilted horizon. Stabilizes to level frame at 2s with subtle deceleration."

### 11. Scale Impossibility
**Setup:** Tiny human in impossibly vast landscape (desert, ocean, space, void), OR a giant object in a confined space. Distance, leading lines, and proportion sell the scale.
**Why it works:** Awe response, or claustrophobia. Both freeze the scroll.
**Phrasing:** "Tiny human figure 1% of frame, vast desert valley filling remaining 99%. Mountains receding to horizon over 5 layered atmospheric planes. Scale realization at ~5s mark."

### 12. Dutch Angle Hold
**Setup:** Frame tilted 20-25 degrees counterclockwise. Horizon diagonal across the frame. Tilt holds throughout the opening 2s without correction.
**Why it works:** Signals "psychological imbalance / danger / dream state" before any narrative content arrives.
**Phrasing:** "Frame tilted 25 degrees counterclockwise from start. Horizon diagonal. Tilt held throughout hook duration. Conveys unease, dreamlike threat."

## Hook stacking

For maximum impact, **layer 2-3 hooks in the opening 2 seconds**. Three field-tested combos:

- **Triple sensory engagement:** Black screen (silence) + color burst (audio drop) + extreme close-up reveal. Used in noir / dramatic openers.
- **Layered attention capture:** Reverse motion (cognitive) + eyes opening (primal) + geometric contrast (compositional). Used in thriller / sci-fi.
- **Awe-prime:** Extreme macro snap + scale impossibility + silent-then-orchestral-swell. Used in epic landscape / nature openers.

When stacking, the second and third hooks land at 0.5s, 1.0s, 1.5s — never simultaneously. Each beat needs its own moment to register.

## Anti-patterns

- **Slow establishing shot as the hook.** "Gorgeous mountain vista pans for 4 seconds" loses 80% of mobile viewers before the subject arrives. Establishing shots come AFTER the hook.
- **Dialogue in the first 2s.** Words take 1-2s to parse. Image and sound are instant.
- **Soft cuts and fades into the open.** A fade-in tells the viewer "you have time." They don't have time. Hard-cut into the hook frame.
- **Ambient music with no drop.** Continuous music bed without a marked downbeat at the hook moment kills the audio half of the contrast.
