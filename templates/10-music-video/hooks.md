# Music Video Hooks — First 2 Seconds

The hook is the visual that lands at or before t=2s and makes the viewer not scroll. In short-form (TikTok/Reels/Shorts), a music video without a hook in the first 2 seconds is dead on arrival regardless of how good the chorus visual is.

Every hook below is paired with a **musical anchor** — the audio event the visual must align to. If your track doesn't contain that anchor at t≤2s, pick a different hook.

---

## 1. Whip-In Reveal

**Musical anchor:** First downbeat after a 1–2 beat silent or muted intro.
**Visual:** Camera whip-pans (motion blur, ~3–5 frames) from black/empty into a sharply-focused subject. Subject lands locked on the downbeat.
**Why it works:** Whip blur primes the eye for impact; the snap to focus on the beat creates an audio-visual handshake.
**Prompt cue:** `"opening 0:00–0:02: rapid whip-pan motion blur from darkness, snapping to a locked-off close-up of [subject] precisely on the first kick drum at 0:02"`
**Genre fit:** All. Especially hip-hop, pop, rock.

## 2. Beat-Drop Reveal

**Musical anchor:** A bass drop or downbeat that lands at t=1.0–2.0s after a brief build.
**Visual:** Dark / silent / minimal frame, then on the drop: full color floods in, particles burst outward, subject hits a power pose.
**Why it works:** Maximum contrast — visual silence to visual peak — mapped to audio silence to audio peak.
**Prompt cue:** `"first 1.5s near-black with low rumble visible as faint warm particles; on the drop at 0:01.5, screen floods with magenta and gold light, particles explode outward, subject snaps into hero pose"`
**Genre fit:** EDM, hip-hop, rock, pop.

## 3. Slow-Mo Entrance

**Musical anchor:** Sustained chord, pad, or held vocal in the intro before tempo locks in.
**Visual:** Subject moves through frame in 50% slow motion — hair flips, fabric trails, water droplets, dust motes lit by a hard side light. Snaps to real-time on the first tempo-locked beat.
**Why it works:** Lets the held audio breathe; the tempo lock-in is the punchline.
**Prompt cue:** `"0:00–0:02 slow motion at 50%: subject walks toward camera through golden backlit haze, hair drifting; at 0:02 on the first kick, frame snaps to real-time with a sharp inhale of camera shake"`
**Genre fit:** R&B, pop, rock, country.

## 4. Single-Frame Flash Reveal

**Musical anchor:** Sharp transient — snare crack, clap, vocal stab, gunshot sample.
**Visual:** Black frame for 1.5s, then a single bright frame of the subject at t=2s, then black again, then sustained on the next beat. Three-flash structure.
**Why it works:** Subliminal — viewer doesn't consciously process the flash but is hooked. Common in horror and trailer cuts.
**Prompt cue:** `"first 1.5s pure black; at 0:01.5 a single 1-frame flash of subject's face in harsh white light synced to the snare; black again until 0:02 when subject sustains in frame under the same light"`
**Genre fit:** Rock, metal, dark hip-hop, horror-pop.

## 5. Top-Down Push (Drone Drop)

**Musical anchor:** Building build-up that releases on a downbeat.
**Visual:** Aerial top-down shot of subject lying / standing centered. Camera pushes straight down toward subject. Push velocity scales with build intensity. Lands on the downbeat with subject filling frame.
**Why it works:** Forced perspective creates inevitability that mirrors the build.
**Prompt cue:** `"top-down aerial: subject lying on concrete in chalked circle, camera at 30m altitude. Camera pushes straight down at accelerating speed during the build, landing tight on subject's face at 0:02 on the downbeat"`
**Genre fit:** Hip-hop, EDM, K-pop.

## 6. Lyric-Title Smash

**Musical anchor:** First lyric line.
**Visual:** Frame opens on bold typography of the song's title or first line (large kerning, brand-heavy color from the genre palette). On the first sung syllable, the type explodes / shatters / wipes off, revealing the subject behind it.
**Why it works:** Type as gate — establishes title and identity before any scene starts.
**Prompt cue:** `"frame opens at 0:00 with massive title 'GHOSTS' in cracked-stone serif filling the frame; at 0:02 on the first sung syllable, the type shatters into ash particles and reveals subject standing in a fog-lit alley"`
**Genre fit:** Pop, rock, hip-hop with strong title hooks. Avoid lo-fi (too loud).

## 7. Inverted World

**Musical anchor:** Reversed/processed audio in the intro that resolves on the first clean beat.
**Visual:** Frame is upside-down or color-inverted (negative). On the audio resolve, frame flips upright / colors snap to normal.
**Why it works:** Audio weirdness has a visual cause. Reward for paying attention.
**Prompt cue:** `"0:00–0:02 frame is fully color-inverted (greens become magentas, skin becomes blue) over a reversed-cymbal swell; at 0:02 on the first kick, colors snap to true and frame stabilizes on subject in their actual environment"`
**Genre fit:** Indie, alt-rock, experimental electronic, lo-fi-with-edge.

## 8. Crowd-Eye POV

**Musical anchor:** Crowd noise / arena reverb in the intro that gives way to the track.
**Visual:** First-person POV from inside a crowd looking up at a stage. Out-of-focus heads frame the bottom; stage lights flare at the top. Subject appears center-stage as the lights cut to them on the downbeat.
**Why it works:** Puts viewer in the show before the show starts.
**Prompt cue:** `"POV inside concert crowd, anonymous heads silhouetted in foreground, stage lights flaring overhead. At 0:02 on the first kick, all stage lights cut to a single white spot on the subject, who is mid-vocal"`
**Genre fit:** Rock, pop, hip-hop, K-pop. Performance mode only.

## 9. Glitch-Stutter Open

**Musical anchor:** Stuttered vocal chop, glitch-edit drum pattern, or DSP artifact in the intro.
**Visual:** First 2s of frame stutters / repeats / tears horizontally / has VHS-style chroma break. Resolves to a clean shot on the first sustained beat.
**Why it works:** Audio glitch + visual glitch = legible style statement. Telegraphs experimental / alt genre.
**Prompt cue:** `"0:00–0:02 frame violently glitches with horizontal RGB tearing, frame duplicates, and chroma offset, synced to a stuttered vocal chop. At 0:02 on the resolve, glitches collapse and frame holds clean on subject"`
**Genre fit:** Hyperpop, glitch-hop, alt-electronic, vaporwave-adjacent.

## 10. Match-Cut From Reference

**Musical anchor:** Single piano note, single guitar strum, or held vocal note.
**Visual:** Frame opens on a still object (a record on a turntable, a guitar pedal lighting up, a hand on a mic). Object's motion match-cuts to the same shape in a wider context — record becomes a spinning camera move, pedal becomes a stage spot, mic becomes performer's mouth.
**Why it works:** Visual misdirection that pays off the audio cue.
**Prompt cue:** `"open on extreme close-up of a vinyl record dropping onto a turntable; at 0:02 on the first piano note, match-cut to a wide shot of the same circular shape — now a stage spotlight pointed down at the subject from above"`
**Genre fit:** Hip-hop, R&B, jazz, indie.

## 11. Color-Bath Wipe

**Musical anchor:** Synth pad swell or pad wash that rises across the first 2 seconds.
**Visual:** Frame starts in monochrome (single hue, low saturation). As the pad swells, color floods in from one edge — a wipe of saturation across the frame. Resolves on the first beat with full palette.
**Why it works:** Visual saturation curve = audio saturation curve.
**Prompt cue:** `"0:00 frame is full monochrome blue, low saturation; over 2s a wave of warm magenta and gold saturates across from left to right, matching the rising synth pad. At 0:02 on the first beat, full chromatic palette holds"`
**Genre fit:** Synthwave, pop, lo-fi, dream-pop.

## 12. Hold-Then-Hit (Anti-Hook)

**Musical anchor:** A track that intentionally delays its first beat by 3–5s after silence.
**Visual:** Hold a single static, slightly unsettling frame for 2s of pure silence — no movement, no music, no caption. Then on the late-arriving first beat, the frame snaps to a totally different scene with full energy.
**Why it works:** Algorithmic risk — viewers may scroll. But for the ones who stay, the payoff hits harder than any conventional hook. Use only for established creators or strong narrative concepts.
**Prompt cue:** `"0:00–0:02 absolute silence, hold on a static medium shot of an empty hallway, single overhead bulb flickering. At 0:02–0:03 on the first kick, hard cut to the chorus visual at full energy"`
**Genre fit:** Cinematic pop, alt-rock, narrative-mode only. **Risky for cold-audience short-form.**

## Picking a hook

Quick decision flow:

1. **Does the track have an obvious anchor in the first 2s?** (kick, drop, snare, lyric, pad). If yes, the anchor type usually picks the hook for you.
2. **What's the strategy mode?** Performance → 1, 2, 3, 8. Narrative → 5, 6, 10, 12. Abstract → 4, 7, 9, 11.
3. **What's the genre?** Cross-check against per-hook "genre fit" tag.
4. **Is this short-form (≤30s)?** Avoid #12. Use #1, #2, #4 for highest retention.
5. **Has the user supplied a reference music video?** Match its hook category, then vary on color/genre/subject.
