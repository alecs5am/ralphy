# CGI Architecture — Opening Hooks (first 2 seconds)

The architecture-social scroll dies in two seconds. The viewer has 80 design accounts in their feed and a coffee that is getting cold. Your opening must plant a single involuntary thought: *"Wait — what is this place?"*

Pick one hook per cut. Don't stack two. The hook is a frame-0 + 1.5s of camera-move commitment, not a montage. Every hook below is paired with the slow camera move that earns it — speed is a defect.

---

## 1. Golden-hour drone push-in

**Frame 0**: high overhead, building centered in landscape — coastline, forest, desert, urban grid, or hill — all visible as context. Sun at 30–45° azimuth, long shadows raking across the facade.
**Move (5s clip)**: drone descends and pushes inward at 0.5 m/s, ending on the building filling the upper two-thirds of frame.
**Setting fit**: estates, hillside houses, hotels, conceptual pavilions, anything where landscape integration is the argument.
**Time of day**: golden hour, 30 minutes before sunset.
**Camera move**: drone push-in, slow descent, no zoom.

---

## 2. Threshold pass at doorway

**Frame 0**: heavy front door closed, golden-hour light raking across material grain — board-form concrete, charred cedar, oak slab, bronze panel. Door is the entire frame.
**Move**: door swings inward (or camera dollies through if no door movement), threshold crosses at 2.5s, the foyer or sight-line through to a courtyard or far view fills frame.
**Setting fit**: every building type. The most universally usable hook for archviz.
**Time of day**: golden hour outside, soft interior fill on the other side.
**Camera move**: threshold walk-through at 0.7 m/s, dolly forward only.

---

## 3. Sunlight cut through floor-to-ceiling glass

**Frame 0**: interior corner of a double-height room. Floor-to-ceiling glazing on one side throws a hard rectangle of sunlight onto polished concrete floor. The shadow line is the composition.
**Move**: micro-dolly forward; the rectangle of light grows; dust motes catch in the rays; the sun-edge crawls across the floor at the back.
**Setting fit**: modernist single-family, conceptual pavilions, museums, galleries.
**Time of day**: late afternoon, sun at 25–35° azimuth.
**Camera move**: dolly-push-in at 0.3 m/s, no rotation.

---

## 4. Pull-back reveal of facade

**Frame 0**: tight on a single architectural moment — a cantilever, a window mullion, a board-form joint, a brass hinge. Material reads close.
**Move**: slow pull-back at 0.4 m/s; the detail recedes; the full elevation blooms around it; the building reveals its scale and composition.
**Setting fit**: high-end residential, hospitality, anything where craftsmanship is the argument.
**Time of day**: bright indirect daylight or golden hour.
**Camera move**: dolly-pull-back, level, no tilt.

---

## 5. Top-down on rooftop terrace

**Frame 0**: drone directly overhead, rooftop terrace as the entire frame — pool, deck, planters, lounge, all reading as a graphic plan view.
**Move**: drone descends and slowly tilts forward; the plan view becomes a perspective view; the horizon enters frame at 4s; the rooftop now reads as a place, not a diagram.
**Setting fit**: penthouses, hotels, restaurants, public buildings with active rooftops.
**Time of day**: golden hour or dusk for warm tonal contrast.
**Camera move**: drone descent + tilt-down-to-tilt-forward.

---

## 6. Through-foliage architectural reveal

**Frame 0**: dense foliage in the foreground — palm fronds, birch trunks, olive branches — heavy, dappled. Building barely visible behind.
**Move**: lateral dolly at 0.4 m/s; the foliage parts; the building is revealed from a new angle, framed by the leaves.
**Setting fit**: tropical-luxe, forest cabins, courtyard houses, anything with mature landscaping.
**Time of day**: golden hour for warm dapple, or bright indirect for clean dapple.
**Camera move**: lateral gimbal glide, level, slight forward drift.

---

## 7. Reflection in pool

**Frame 0**: water surface in the foreground filling the lower half of frame; the building's reflection on the water is the upper composition; a still planar surface, no ripple.
**Move**: micro-dolly forward; a ripple enters frame at 2s and crosses the reflection; the building distorts and re-forms; the camera ends with the actual building rising into upper frame above the water.
**Setting fit**: waterfront homes, infinity pools, courtyard reflecting pools, hotel resorts, museums with water features.
**Time of day**: golden hour or dusk — the reflection carries the sky color.
**Camera move**: dolly-forward, level, gradual rise at 4s.

---

## 8. Night exterior with interior glow

**Frame 0**: exterior at full dark, sky deep teal-blue (not black). Interior practicals are on — warm tungsten 2700K visible through every glazing element. The building reads as a lantern in landscape.
**Move**: slow lateral glide along the facade; interior tableaux become visible through different windows in sequence; the camera ends on the room with the most interior life.
**Setting fit**: any high-value residential, hospitality, restaurant, or boutique retail.
**Time of day**: 30–45 minutes after sunset; sky still has color.
**Camera move**: gimbal lateral glide at 0.4 m/s.

---

## 9. Detail of staircase / handrail / material join

**Frame 0**: extreme close on a craftsmanship moment — a monolithic stair tread, a brass handrail meeting an oak post, a plaster wall meeting a terrazzo floor. Material physics read precisely.
**Move**: slow tilt-up or rack-focus, so the detail unfolds across the clip; the eye is led from one craft moment to the next; the wide context arrives in the last second.
**Setting fit**: design-led residential, boutique hotels, art galleries, high-end retail.
**Time of day**: bright indirect, slight directional sun for material rake.
**Camera move**: rack-focus on materiality; or slow tilt-up at 4°/sec.

---

## 10. Skyline through window frame

**Frame 0**: dim interior of a high-floor room. Floor-to-ceiling glass at the far end. The city skyline is bright value beyond — distant towers, evening haze.
**Move**: dolly-push-in at 0.3 m/s toward the glass; the skyline gradually fills the frame; the room recedes into shadow on either side; the architecture *frames* the view rather than competing with it.
**Setting fit**: penthouses, urban hotels, towers, high-rise residential.
**Time of day**: dusk or twilight for skyline contrast.
**Camera move**: dolly-push-in toward window.

---

## 11. Diagonal cross-section reveal

**Frame 0**: an angled diagonal slice through the building — visible interior on one side, exterior on the other, as if the architect's section drawing came to life. Common in conceptual archviz.
**Move**: slow orbit around the section line at 5°/sec; both halves of the building rotate into and out of frame; the spatial relationship becomes legible.
**Setting fit**: conceptual pavilions, competition entries, student portfolios, firm capabilities reels.
**Time of day**: bright indirect or golden hour for material contrast.
**Camera move**: slow orbit, level.

---

## 12. Material-comparison sweep

**Frame 0**: a long elevation or interior wall where multiple materials meet — board-form concrete next to oak millwork next to terrazzo, or brass next to leather next to plaster. Each material is its own panel.
**Move**: lateral dolly at 0.4 m/s along the wall; the camera passes each material in sequence; light direction shifts subtly so each material's character pops as the camera reaches it.
**Setting fit**: design-pitch reels, materiality-focused projects, designer collaborations.
**Time of day**: bright indirect, slight rake for grain pop.
**Camera move**: gimbal lateral glide, level, no tilt.

---

## Picking the right hook

- **If the project has a strong site / landscape** → hook 1, 2, 6. Lead with context.
- **If the project's argument is light and space** → hook 3, 5, 10. Lead with light.
- **If the project's argument is materiality and craft** → hook 4, 9, 12. Lead with material.
- **If the project has a signature moment** (pool, courtyard, double-height) → hook 7 or 8 if water/glow, hook 9 if a built detail.
- **If the project is conceptual / sectional / academic** → hook 11. Section is the language.
- **If the project is mid-portfolio without a hero feature** → hook 2 (threshold) is the safest. It works on every building type.

Do not pick a hook the project does not actually have. The hook fails the moment the AI invents a feature absent from the brief — a pool that is not in the program, a forest the site is not in. Match the brief; if the brief is thin, ask before generating.
