# E-commerce Ad — Hook Library

The first 2 seconds decide whether the viewer scrolls past or watches to purchase. These 12 patterns are tested across categories. Pick ONE per ad — combining hooks dilutes both.

For each: **setup**, **why it works**, **example**, and the **categories it fits best**.

---

## 1. Hero Drop with Dramatic Impact

**Setup.** Dark, minimal background. Product descends into frame with motion blur and a lighting shift as it lands. Often paired with a low whoosh or impact sound.

**Why.** Instantly signals "premium," "new arrival," "this matters." The motion is unusual enough to interrupt scroll-momentum.

**Example.** Luxury watch falling onto black silk, catching key light mid-drop. Headphones descending against deep navy with subtle blue rim light.

**Best for.** Electronics, jewelry, fragrance, premium fashion, automotive accessories.

---

## 2. Satisfying Texture ASMR Close-Up

**Setup.** Macro shot of product texture. Fingers gliding across fabric weave, light raking over metal grain, finger pressing into clay mask, drop of serum hitting glass.

**Why.** Triggers sensory engagement. Compensates for the fact that buyers can't touch online. Pause-rate goes up sharply on satisfying texture.

**Example.** Index finger sliding across velvet, revealing a stitched logo beneath. Macro of serum dropping into amber glass, slow ripple.

**Best for.** Beauty, skincare, fashion (fabric), home (linens, ceramics), food (sauce, melt, drip).

---

## 3. Before / After Transformation

**Setup.** Split-screen or quick cut: problem state → solved state. Same person, same framing, only the product changed.

**Why.** Communicates benefit in two seconds. Answers "what's in it for me?" without a single word.

**Example.** Dull, tired skin → glowing skin (skincare). Cluttered desk → organized desk (organizer). Limp hair → bouncy hair (haircare).

**Best for.** Beauty, skincare, fitness, home organization, haircare, cleaning products.

---

## 4. Stop-Scroll Direct Address

**Setup.** Person looks straight into the camera, makes a hand gesture (palm-up "stop", finger-point), or breaks the fourth wall with an expression. Often paired with text "Wait, watch this."

**Why.** The eye-contact + gesture combo registers as a real human signaling, not an ad. Pattern-interrupt.

**Example.** Creator hand-up to camera + overlay "You need to see this." Person leaning in toward camera with raised eyebrow.

**Best for.** Any category with a creator-led approach. Especially beauty, fashion, food, fitness.

---

## 5. Unboxing Reveal

**Setup.** Hands open premium packaging. Box lifts, tissue parts, product emerges into clean light. Slow enough to register the packaging quality.

**Why.** Taps into the unboxing-ASMR genre that already has hundreds of millions of dedicated viewers. Premium packaging signals premium product.

**Example.** Gold-foil box opening to reveal a skincare bottle nested in cream tissue. Magnetic-close box lifting on a watch with audible click.

**Best for.** Beauty, jewelry, electronics, premium gifting categories, subscription boxes.

---

## 6. Color / Variant Cascade

**Setup.** Same SKU in multiple colorways arranged in a line or grid, rotating or flipping in quick succession. 8-12 variants in 2 seconds.

**Why.** Viewers searching for "their color" stop to scan. Communicates range without a word. High completion-rate hook.

**Example.** Lipstick lineup rotating through 12 shades. Phone cases flipping through 9 colors on a grid.

**Best for.** Fashion, beauty (lipstick, polish, eyeshadow), accessories (cases, straps), home textiles.

---

## 7. Ingredient / Material Explosion

**Setup.** Product ingredients or component materials splinter outward from a central point. Botanicals, oils, fabric swatches, metal flakes — visual, dynamic, energetic.

**Why.** Communicates "natural," "premium," "handmade" visually in a way text can't. Earns the "what's in it" beat without a slow ingredient list.

**Example.** Honey, oats, lavender exploding outward from a skincare jar's center. Coffee beans, cocoa nibs, vanilla pods bursting from behind a chocolate bar.

**Best for.** Beauty, skincare, food, supplements, fragrance, candles.

---

## 8. Problem-Solution Snap

**Setup.** Text overlay "Problem:" with a 1-second visual of the frustration → quick cut → "Solution:" with the product, lit clean.

**Why.** Direct, no fluff. The viewer instantly knows what the ad is about and decides whether they have that problem.

**Example.** "Can't find pants that fit" (visual: sad changing-room moment) → product reveal with fit-guarantee text. "Phone always dies" → portable charger.

**Best for.** Fitness, fashion (fit-focused), electronics, automotive accessories, home organization, pet (training problems).

---

## 9. In-Hand Usage Tease

**Setup.** Hands holding the product, demonstrating one key feature in 2 seconds. Not the full demo — a tease.

**Why.** Shows context immediately. The viewer mentally completes the action. "I see how this fits into my life."

**Example.** Phone case dropping, the phone catches it, lands intact. Single resistance band stretched to show give-and-spring.

**Best for.** Electronics, fitness, kitchen tools, toys, automotive accessories, pet products.

---

## 10. Lifestyle Aspiration Flash

**Setup.** Quick cut to a relatable, beautiful lifestyle scene with the product integrated. Warm lighting, natural setting, real-feeling moment.

**Why.** Sells the life, not the product. Emotional hook. Best for products where buying is identity-driven.

**Example.** Morning coffee in a premium mug, sunlight, serene kitchen. Person in the cardigan walking through autumn leaves.

**Best for.** Fashion, home, food/beverage, fragrance, wellness, premium accessories, pet (lifestyle moments with the pet).

---

## 11. Scarcity / Urgency Signal

**Setup.** Text overlay "Limited Drop" / "Only 500 Made" / "48HR Flash Sale" / countdown timer with product glowing or spotlit.

**Why.** FOMO drives click-through and add-to-cart, especially on TikTok Shop and IG. Works best when the scarcity is real or believable.

**Example.** Product with "48HR FLASH SALE" animated text and a visible countdown. Single product on a pedestal with "1 of 500" engraving callout.

**Best for.** Drops, limited-edition releases, seasonal collections, flash sales. Works across all categories.

---

## 12. Creator Reaction

**Setup.** Creator unboxes or first-uses the product. Eyes widen, brief gasp, "oh my god," genuine reaction beat.

**Why.** Authentic emotional response is contagious. Reads as social proof in 2 seconds. The viewer trusts a real reaction more than any claim.

**Example.** Unboxing a beauty box, surprised expression, product reveal. First sip of a coffee, slow nod, "okay that's actually good."

**Best for.** Beauty, food/beverage, toys (with kids), pet (with pets), gifting, subscription boxes. Less effective for technical products (electronics, automotive).

---

## How to pick

| Goal | Hook |
|---|---|
| Premium feel, signal value | 1 (hero drop) or 5 (unboxing) |
| Tactile / sensory product | 2 (texture) or 7 (ingredient) |
| Solves a clear problem | 3 (before/after) or 8 (problem-solution) |
| Range of variants | 6 (cascade) |
| Drive urgency / cart-add | 11 (scarcity) |
| Identity / aspiration purchase | 10 (lifestyle) |
| Creator-led / influencer | 4 (stop-scroll) or 12 (reaction) |
| Demonstrate function fast | 9 (in-hand tease) |

## Mistakes

- **Stacking two hooks.** "Hero drop + before/after" dilutes both. Pick one for the first 2 seconds. The second pattern can land at 3-5s as the showcase begins.
- **Hook is text, not visual.** A title card is not a hook. The hook must be visual movement or an emotional beat.
- **Hook doesn't match category.** Creator-reaction on automotive parts feels off. Hero-drop on a pet toy feels off. Use the matrix above.
- **Hook gives away the whole ad.** A hook teases; it does not summarize. Save the full demo for the showcase beat.
