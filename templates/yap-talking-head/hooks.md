# Hooks — YAP headline patterns

The 0-1s headline overlay is the most load-bearing single asset in a YAP video. Below are 12 patterns that consistently drop on Hormozi, Codie Sanchez, Max Tornow, Justin Welsh, and the broader business-finance-education YAP cohort. Each pattern includes:

- **Setup** — the rhetorical move the headline performs.
- **Niche fit** — where it lands hardest.
- **Headline design** — typographic spec for the Remotion overlay.

The patterns are not interchangeable. Pick the one whose rhetorical move matches the idea, not the one that "looks coolest."

---

## 1. "Why your X is wrong"

**Setup.** Direct attack on the viewer's current belief. Frames the video as a corrective, which buys ~3-5s of grace before the viewer needs the payoff.
**Niche fit.** Finance, business, productivity, fitness — anywhere there's conventional wisdom to invert. Avoid for soft niches (mindset, lifestyle) — too aggressive.
**Headline design.** Single line, 96-112px, Inter Black, white on transparent (or yellow on black). Top third of frame. Hold 0-1s, fade out 6 frames.

> "Why your savings strategy is wrong"
> "Why your morning routine is wrong"
> "Why your pricing is wrong"

---

## 2. "The truth about X"

**Setup.** Promises insider information. Lower aggression than #1, higher curiosity. Reads as "I'll tell you what nobody tells you."
**Niche fit.** Education, finance, business, dating, fitness. Universal — works across niches.
**Headline design.** Two lines. Line 1 small ("THE TRUTH ABOUT") in 56-72px tracking-loose. Line 2 large (the topic) in 112-128px Black. Center-top.

> "THE TRUTH ABOUT
> compound interest"
>
> "THE TRUTH ABOUT
> hiring your first employee"

---

## 3. "If you're [doing X], stop"

**Setup.** Imperative + specificity. The "if you're" filter immediately self-selects an audience and the "stop" lands as a directive. High retention because viewers in the bucket need to know why.
**Niche fit.** Finance, fitness, productivity, business operations. Less effective in mindset (too prescriptive).
**Headline design.** Single line, 96-104px Black, 2-tone — "If you're" in 60% opacity, the action and "stop" in full white. Top third.

> "If you're saving 10% of your income, stop."
> "If you're cold-emailing one-by-one, stop."
> "If you're tracking macros to the gram, stop."

---

## 4. "[N] minutes of [topic]"

**Setup.** Time-boxed promise. Reads as a mini-podcast clip. Lowers the perceived cost of watching ("only 2 minutes") even on a 50s video.
**Niche fit.** Education, finance, business, mindset. Especially good for "evergreen" knowledge content.
**Headline design.** Two-tone single line. Number large (128-160px black), word "minutes of" small (48-64px), topic large (112-128px). Center.

> "**2 minutes** of pricing strategy"
> "**90 seconds** on Roth conversions"
> "**3 minutes** of hiring advice"

---

## 5. "Stop doing X if you want Y"

**Setup.** Imperative + outcome. Sharper than #3 because it names the goal explicitly. Works when there's a counterintuitive habit-to-outcome link.
**Niche fit.** Finance, fitness, productivity, business. Pattern-tired in 2026 — use sparingly and only when the link is genuinely surprising.
**Headline design.** Single line, 88-96px (slightly smaller than #1/#3 because the line is longer). Top third.

> "Stop budgeting if you want to save more"
> "Stop chasing leads if you want clients"
> "Stop doing cardio if you want to lose fat"

---

## 6. "I made $X doing this — here's how"

**Setup.** Specific number + promise of method. The number is the credibility lever. Use real, defensible numbers only — fabricated numbers are the #1 reason YAP videos get reported and shadow-banned in 2026.
**Niche fit.** Business, finance, freelance / creator economy. Inappropriate elsewhere.
**Headline design.** Two lines. Line 1: "I MADE $XK" in 128-160px black, dollar amount color-shifted (green or yellow). Line 2: "here's how" in 64-80px italic. Top third.

> "**I MADE $40K**
> *here's how*"
>
> "**I MADE $1.2M ON ONE EMAIL**
> *here's how*"

---

## 7. "X is the new Y"

**Setup.** Reframe pattern. Repositions a familiar concept as obsolete and proposes a replacement. Strong save-rate because it gives viewers a sharable thesis.
**Niche fit.** Business, marketing, finance, technology. Weak in fitness / mindset (those domains don't have "new vs old" the same way).
**Headline design.** Single line, 96-112px, with the two nouns in different colors (X yellow, Y white, "is the new" small and gray).

> "Newsletters are the new podcasts"
> "Pricing is the new marketing"
> "Boring is the new viral"

---

## 8. "Nobody talks about X"

**Setup.** Insider-claim. Promises information the viewer hasn't heard. Higher curiosity than #2 but lower trust — viewers read it as a slight overclaim. Works when the content actually delivers an underdiscussed point.
**Niche fit.** Finance (tax tactics, niche instruments), business (operational tactics), education (career-path specifics).
**Headline design.** Two lines. Line 1: "NOBODY TALKS ABOUT" in 56-72px tracking-loose. Line 2: topic in 120-144px Black. Top third.

> "NOBODY TALKS ABOUT
> S-corp distributions"
>
> "NOBODY TALKS ABOUT
> firing your first hire"

---

## 9. "X people don't tell you about Y"

**Setup.** Authority-attribution. Names a group ("rich people," "founders," "doctors") and claims they withhold information. Heavy curiosity lift. Read as conspiratorial in 2026 unless the group is concrete and the withhold is plausible.
**Niche fit.** Finance, business, health-adjacent education. Avoid in mindset (sounds like a course-seller).
**Headline design.** Three lines, smaller weight overall. Line 1: "[GROUP]" small caps 72px. Line 2: "DON'T TELL YOU ABOUT" 56px. Line 3: topic 112-128px black.

> "FOUNDERS
> DON'T TELL YOU ABOUT
> the first 90 days"

---

## 10. "Read this before you X"

**Setup.** Imperative + gating action. Frames the video as preparation for a decision the viewer is already considering. High retention because viewers in the decision-bucket can't afford to swipe.
**Niche fit.** Finance (before you invest, before you refinance), business (before you hire, before you raise), career (before you quit).
**Headline design.** Two lines. Line 1: "READ THIS BEFORE" 64-80px tracking-loose. Line 2: action 112-128px Black with action verb italicized.

> "READ THIS BEFORE
> *you raise a round*"
>
> "READ THIS BEFORE
> *you quit your job*"

---

## 11. "The X mistake that costs you Y"

**Setup.** Specific pain point + concrete cost. The dual specificity lifts both relevance and stakes. Works best when the cost is quantified ("$10K," "5 years," "your first hire").
**Niche fit.** Finance, business, hiring, productivity, fitness. Universal but most potent in money-adjacent niches.
**Headline design.** Single line OR two lines, depending on length. 96-112px Black. Cost in contrast color (red or yellow) for emphasis.

> "The hiring mistake that costs you **$50K**"
> "The pricing mistake that costs you **clients**"
> "The 1 mistake that costs founders **months**"

---

## 12. "X in [Y seconds / Y words]"

**Setup.** Tiny, deliverable promise. Pairs perfectly with the YAP format because the format itself is short. Reads as "I'll be quick about this." Often the highest completion rate of any pattern.
**Niche fit.** Education, finance, business. Less suited to fitness / mindset (those need more breathing room).
**Headline design.** Single line, 104-128px Black. Number + word ("30 SECONDS" or "10 WORDS") in contrast color. Top third.

> "Roth IRAs in **30 seconds**"
> "Series A in **10 words**"
> "Compounding in **45 seconds**"

---

## Pattern selection cheat sheet

| If the idea is... | Use pattern |
|---|---|
| Inverting conventional wisdom | #1 "Why your X is wrong" |
| Insider knowledge / explainer | #2 "The truth about X" |
| Stop-doing instruction | #3 or #5 |
| Time-boxed mini-podcast | #4 |
| Personal-result method-share (with real number!) | #6 |
| Reframe / category shift | #7 |
| Underdiscussed tactic | #8 |
| Group-knowledge claim | #9 |
| Pre-decision warning | #10 |
| Specific costly mistake | #11 |
| Quick explainer | #12 |

When in doubt, default to #2 ("The truth about X") — it's the most universal and lowest-aggression pattern in the set.
