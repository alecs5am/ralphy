# Hooks — Active Lifestyle

12 openers for the 0-3s establishing beat. Each is a vibe specification, not a literal copy. The hook is *visual* — caption text is optional and almost always omitted in this format. Pair each with the right audio profile (BPM band determines whether the piece reads as driven, serene, or social).

## How to read this file

Each hook lists:
- **Sport fit** — which sports it lands cleanest on.
- **Time-of-day cue** — the lighting that makes it read.
- **Audio profile (BPM range)** — the music brief to send to ElevenLabs Music.
- **Vibe note** — what the establishing shot has to do.

If you find yourself writing on-screen text for the hook, stop and re-read this file — the hook is the camera move, not a caption.

---

## 1. Morning ride before work

- **Sport fit:** cycling, running.
- **Time-of-day cue:** 6:30-7:30am, low golden side-light, breath visible in cool air, empty road or park path.
- **Audio profile:** instrumental electronic, 115-125 BPM, driven but not aggressive — think early-morning podcast-intro energy.
- **Vibe note:** pull-back-to-action from a low angle behind the rear wheel / heel. The activity is *already in progress* when the camera reveals it; we don't see the start. Establishing shot is the cadence.

## 2. Sunset padel

- **Sport fit:** padel, tennis.
- **Time-of-day cue:** 7-8pm summer, long shadows on the court, stadium lights just coming up, sky still warm.
- **Audio profile:** hip-hop / Latin-pop crossover, 100-110 BPM, sun-soaked.
- **Vibe note:** open on the rally already mid-exchange. Camera is courtside-low, tracks one player. Sunglasses on, sweat sheen, ball-pop diegetic. No serve-from-zero — we land on the rally.

## 3. PR attempt today

- **Sport fit:** gym (powerlifting, oly), climbing.
- **Time-of-day cue:** late afternoon gym light through high windows / industrial fluorescent — moody, not bright.
- **Audio profile:** hip-hop or rock-edge, 95-110 BPM, controlled aggression. Not pumped EDM.
- **Vibe note:** slow push-in to the bar / wall. Chalk dust, pre-lift breath ritual, brief stare-down. Establishing shot is the *preparation*, the activity beat is the lift itself in 3-18s.

## 4. Trail run, day [N]

- **Sport fit:** running, hiking.
- **Time-of-day cue:** mid-morning forest light, dappled through canopy, dust kicking off the trail.
- **Audio profile:** acoustic indie / driving-acoustic, 90-105 BPM, momentum without urgency.
- **Vibe note:** camera handheld, low, tracks alongside the runner from below knee height for 2 seconds — feet, dust, root, then pulls back to reveal the full stride. Smart-watch glance at 8-10s.

## 5. Recovery day

- **Sport fit:** yoga, light running, hiking, post-gym.
- **Time-of-day cue:** soft midday in a sunlit room / park / quiet pool deck. No harsh shadows.
- **Audio profile:** ambient / piano-led / lo-fi, 70-90 BPM.
- **Vibe note:** the establishing shot is *stillness*. Slow flow, slow breath, slow walk. Product placement is recovery-coded: foam roller, recovery drink, compression boots, water bottle being refilled.

## 6. Swell's coming in

- **Sport fit:** surfing, paddleboarding.
- **Time-of-day cue:** dawn or dusk, glassy water, low sun on the horizon.
- **Audio profile:** acoustic / atmospheric indie, 80-100 BPM, oceanic — think slow-build with breathing-room.
- **Vibe note:** open on the water, board waxing or paddle-out already in motion. Sunscreen on the cheek, sunglasses pushed up. The wave is the second beat, not the first.

## 7. First hike of [season]

- **Sport fit:** hiking, trail running, ski-touring (winter variant).
- **Time-of-day cue:** crisp morning, mountain air, frost or new-spring green depending on the season.
- **Audio profile:** cinematic acoustic / folk-electronic crossover, 90-110 BPM, expansive.
- **Vibe note:** wide-establishing pull-back from the boots on the trail to reveal the landscape behind the hiker. The product (boots, jacket logo, smart-watch) is in this very first frame. The reveal is the *scale*, not the gear.

## 8. Long-run training

- **Sport fit:** running (half-marathon / marathon training arc).
- **Time-of-day cue:** late afternoon golden, urban or park loop. Long shadows in stride.
- **Audio profile:** instrumental electronic, 120-128 BPM, locked-in steady.
- **Vibe note:** the cadence *is* the hook. Camera at hip-height, tracking dolly alongside, locked on the stride for 3 seconds before any cut. Foot strike, watch face, breath rhythm. This is the most cadence-dependent hook in the set.

## 9. Silent establishing shot

- **Sport fit:** any — universal opener.
- **Time-of-day cue:** depends on the activity. Match the natural time for the sport.
- **Audio profile:** music drops in at frame 30-45 (1-1.5s) after a brief diegetic-only opener (chain click, ball bounce, rack clank, ocean lap, footstrike on gravel).
- **Vibe note:** the most-restrained opener in the set. Pure sport-sound for the first second — the music landing *is* the hook. Use this when the sport is highly recognizable and doesn't need framing (a clearly-iconic court / road / wave / barbell).

## 10. Pre-game ritual

- **Sport fit:** padel, tennis, basketball, gym.
- **Time-of-day cue:** locker room / car / bench, indoor mixed light, before the activity.
- **Audio profile:** hip-hop, 100-110 BPM, focused.
- **Vibe note:** the activity hasn't started yet. We're in the prep beat — taping fingers, lacing shoes, sipping the pre-workout, grip-tape inspection, glance at the watch. The 3-18s activity beat is the actual game. Product placement is heaviest here — bag, shoes, drink, watch — because the hands are slow and deliberate.

## 11. Golden-hour lap

- **Sport fit:** cycling, running, swimming (open-water).
- **Time-of-day cue:** 7-8pm summer, sun low, long warm light raking across the rider/runner.
- **Audio profile:** electronic / synth-driven, 115-125 BPM, euphoric-but-not-pumped.
- **Vibe note:** drone-style pull-back establishing shot — camera rises and reveals the road / coast / track stretching out. Flare bias intentional. Product (bottle, jersey logo, watch) in the first 2 seconds.

## 12. After the climb

- **Sport fit:** cycling (climb summit), hiking, ski-touring.
- **Time-of-day cue:** late morning at altitude, thin clear light, valley below.
- **Audio profile:** cinematic acoustic / piano-led, 80-95 BPM, satisfaction-coded.
- **Vibe note:** this hook *inverts the arc* — opens on the satisfaction beat (heavy breath at the top, hands on knees, view of the valley), then the 3-18s beat is the *flashback* of the climb. Use sparingly; it's the most stylized hook in the set, but it lands hard for outdoor gear and recovery products.

## Picking a hook

- **Driven-sport brief (cycling, running, gym, padel competitive)** → 1, 3, 8, 10, 11.
- **Serene-flow brief (yoga, hiking, recovery, surf, ski-touring)** → 5, 6, 7, 12.
- **Universal / iconic-sport** → 9.
- **Sun-bias brand (sunglasses, sunscreen, summer apparel)** → 2, 6, 11.
- **Ritual-heavy product (supplement, watch, shoes)** → 10.
