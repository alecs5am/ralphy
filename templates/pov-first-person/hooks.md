# Hooks — pov-first-person

12 POV opener patterns. Each one is a frame for the text overlay that lands in the first 2 seconds. Pick one per project, fill in the specific scenario, and write the payoff to match. Don't paste verbatim — the patterns are armature, not script.

The overlay is rendered as a separate centered text element (top-third or middle), big, white with stroke or shadow. It stays on screen for the full hook beat (≥ 2s). It is NOT part of the spoken VO captions — handle it as a static overlay with `<AbsoluteFill>` + `<H1>` in Remotion.

---

## 1. `POV: you just <discovered / realized / found out> X`

**Setup.** The viewer is mid-discovery. Reaction is shock / awe / dread / curiosity.
**Niches.** Comedy, lifestyle, dating, B2B-SaaS.
**Why it stops scroll.** The verb implies a story the viewer wants to see resolved. Curiosity gap.
**Example.** `POV: you just discovered your new manager went to the same college as your ex.`

---

## 2. `POV: it's <year / time / situation> and you're <doing X>`

**Setup.** Anchors the viewer in a specific moment in time or life. Often nostalgic or dystopian.
**Niches.** Comedy, lifestyle, retail (era-fashion), parenting.
**Why it stops scroll.** Time-anchor activates memory. Viewer projects their own version of that moment.
**Example.** `POV: it's 2014 and you just got tagged in a Facebook photo without warning.`

---

## 3. `POV: your <job / relationship / family / boss> is <X>`

**Setup.** Names a recurring frustration or quirk. Reaction is fed-up / deadpan / resigned.
**Niches.** B2B-SaaS, comedy, dating, parenting, retail (employee POV).
**Why it stops scroll.** Universally recognizable category — everyone has a job / relationship / family.
**Example.** `POV: your only marketer at a startup and the founder just said "let's go viral by Friday."`

---

## 4. `POV: coming home <after / to> X`

**Setup.** Implies a transition from one emotional state to another. Often relief or dread.
**Niches.** Lifestyle, parenting, dating, B2B (after-work decompression).
**Why it stops scroll.** "Coming home" is universal — viewer sees the hallway, the door, the couch.
**Example.** `POV: coming home after a 2-hour customer call that should've been an email.`

---

## 5. `POV: you're the <only / first / last> <X> in <Y>`

**Setup.** Isolation / identity beat. Reaction is wistful, defiant, or comic.
**Niches.** B2B-SaaS, comedy, dating, lifestyle.
**Why it stops scroll.** Specific identity claim — viewer either is that person or knows that person.
**Example.** `POV: you're the only marketer at a startup and you just got asked "what does marketing even do."`

---

## 6. `POV: you <texted / sent / posted / replied> to the wrong <person / chat / thread>`

**Setup.** The classic dread beat. Reaction is panicked, frozen, then resigned.
**Niches.** Comedy, dating, B2B (Slack / email scenarios).
**Why it stops scroll.** Universal anxiety. Viewer's stomach drops in solidarity.
**Example.** `POV: you just sent the "ugh I hate this guy" message to the actual guy.`

---

## 7. `POV: <a specific small thing> just happened`

**Setup.** A micro-event with disproportionate emotional weight. Tonal anchor: deadpan or wistful.
**Niches.** Lifestyle, comedy, dating, retail (small wins / small losses).
**Why it stops scroll.** Specificity. The viewer either had this exact thing happen or wants to.
**Example.** `POV: the barista just remembered your order without asking.`

---

## 8. `POV: you're <on a date / in a meeting / at a wedding> and X`

**Setup.** Public situation + private reaction. The character is doing public-face acting while internally reacting.
**Niches.** Dating, comedy, B2B, lifestyle.
**Why it stops scroll.** Tension between public composure and private reaction is inherently watchable.
**Example.** `POV: you're on a third date and they just said "I don't really do labels."`

---

## 9. `POV: <a thing the viewer fears> is happening <right now>`

**Setup.** Fear-based hook. Reaction is panic / freeze / dark humor.
**Niches.** Comedy, B2B-SaaS (production-down moment), dating, parenting.
**Why it stops scroll.** Activates the viewer's own anxiety. They watch to see how the character handles it.
**Example.** `POV: prod is down, the founder just walked in, and you haven't pushed in three weeks.`

---

## 10. `POV: <character role>, day <N>`

**Setup.** Series / journal opener. Implies a longer arc, encourages follow.
**Niches.** Lifestyle (fitness journey, recovery, learning), B2B (founder journey), parenting.
**Why it stops scroll.** Series numbering implies a narrative; viewer is curious where it goes.
**Example.** `POV: solo founder, day 47, your only customer just churned.`

---

## 11. `POV: you <said yes / agreed / signed up> to <X> and now <Y>`

**Setup.** Consequences-of-a-choice beat. Reaction is overwhelmed / regretful / surprised-it's-actually-fine.
**Niches.** Comedy, dating, fitness, B2B (took the job).
**Why it stops scroll.** Cause-and-effect implies a payoff. Viewer wants to see the consequence.
**Example.** `POV: you said "I'll just do a quick 5K" and now you're on minute 37 of stretching.`

---

## 12. `POV: <a real-life moment that nobody films> is happening`

**Setup.** Meta-format. Acknowledges the un-filmable moment by filming it.
**Niches.** Comedy, lifestyle, dating, parenting.
**Why it stops scroll.** Recognition + permission. Viewer realizes "yes, this is a thing, and someone finally named it."
**Example.** `POV: you and your partner are in the silent stand-off about who's gonna get up and turn off the light.`

## Hook-pattern cheatsheet

| Pattern # | Verb / structure | Best niches | Default tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | discovered / realized | comedy, B2B, dating | shock / curiosity |
| 2 | it's <year> and you're | comedy, retail, parenting | nostalgic / dystopian |
| 3 | your <X> is | B2B, comedy, parenting | deadpan / fed-up |
| 4 | coming home after | lifestyle, parenting, B2B | weary / relief |
| 5 | only / first / last | B2B, comedy, dating | wistful / defiant |
| 6 | wrong person / chat | comedy, dating, B2B | panicked / frozen |
| 7 | small thing happened | lifestyle, comedy, retail | deadpan / wistful |
| 8 | on a date / meeting | dating, comedy, B2B | public-face acting |
| 9 | fear is happening | comedy, B2B, parenting | panic / dark humor |
| 10 | role, day N | lifestyle, B2B, parenting | sincere / wry |
| 11 | said yes and now | comedy, fitness, B2B | overwhelmed / regretful |
| 12 | un-filmable moment | comedy, lifestyle, dating | meta / wry |

## Selection rule

If the brief gives you the niche, pick from the "best niches" column. If the brief gives you the tone, pick from the "default tone" column. If both, the intersection narrows it to 1-2 patterns and you pick by feel.

If neither — ask. POV format collapses without a specific scenario, and a wrong-pattern hook ships an un-shareable video.
