# Hooks — photo-dump opening text patterns

The opening text overlay is optional but high-leverage: it sets the niche in <1s and gives the algorithm a hookable first frame. Keep it 1-7 words, kerned, low-key sans-serif (or a soft serif for fashion / aesthetic dumps), placed lower-third or top-third to leave the photo's subject visible.

If the dump is silent (no VO), the text appears as a Remotion `<h1>` overlay across the first 1-2 photos and fades out before the chorus snap-cuts begin. If narrated, ElevenLabs reads the line over photo 1 only, then drops out.

Each pattern below: setup → niche fit → example.

---

## 1. "This looks so cool, I have to capture it"

**Setup.** The Later-confirmed trend line of 2025-2026. Reads as a candid in-the-moment thought rather than a caption. Works best when photo 1 is the *thing* being captured (a doorway, a sunset, a plate of food) — the text + image read as a single unit.

**Niche fit.** Travel, aesthetic, lifestyle. Avoid for product dumps (too soft).

**Example.** Photo 1: a peeling-paint Lisbon doorway in golden hour. Text fades in over the lower third, holds for 1.2s, fades out as the kick lands and photo 2 snaps in.

---

## 2. "[Month] dump"

**Setup.** The most universal photo-dump line. Two-three words, present-tense, anchors the dump as a recap. Works in any niche.

**Niche fit.** Lifestyle, travel, fashion, aesthetic, brand week-recap (swap to "[Brand] week" or "Week one"). Universal.

**Example.** "October dump." 14 photos from a single month — coffee shops, walks, one outfit, a dog, a sunset, a meal, a book. Steady pacing, no chorus density.

---

## 3. "An ode to [niche]"

**Setup.** Slightly poetic, signals an aesthetic dump rather than a literal recap. Lets the dump be themed (a season, a city, a feeling) rather than chronological.

**Niche fit.** Aesthetic, travel, fashion. Skip for product / brand dumps (too literary).

**Example.** "An ode to autumn." Photos all share warm-orange palette, golden hour, leaf textures, knitwear. No specific date or location — pure aesthetic.

---

## 4. "What [my week / my trip / my [day]] looked like"

**Setup.** The most narrative opener. Frames the dump as a window into the creator's experience. Works best at 12-16 photos so it feels like a real recap.

**Niche fit.** Lifestyle, travel, brand week-recap, "day in the life" content.

**Example.** "What my week looked like." Monday coffee, Tuesday meeting screenshot, Wednesday walk, Thursday outfit, Friday dinner, Saturday concert, Sunday recovery. One photo per day stretched into 14 photos by adding paired shots.

---

## 5. "[N] things I saw this week"

**Setup.** The "list" framing. Implies curation rather than chronology — the creator is a tasteful filter, not a diarist. Works well for design / fashion / city-living niches.

**Niche fit.** Aesthetic, fashion, lifestyle in dense urban niches (NYC / Tokyo / Lisbon / etc.).

**Example.** "7 things I saw in Tokyo." Seven framed photos with subtle index counters in the corner (photo 1 has "1/7", photo 7 has "7/7"). Doubles as a save-bait.

---

## 6. "Just vibes"

**Setup.** Two-word permission slip. The dump has no theme, no recap, no point — it's pure aesthetic. Counterintuitively one of the highest-saving lines because it sets zero expectation.

**Niche fit.** Aesthetic, lifestyle, fashion. Skip for brand / product dumps.

**Example.** "Just vibes." A loose collection — coffee, light through a window, a passage of a book, a hand on a fabric. No through-line beyond the grade.

---

## 7. "Photo dump because [reason]"

**Setup.** Self-aware framing. The "because" can be sincere ("…because I miss summer") or playful ("…because I'm bored"). Reads as personal, lowers the production-stakes feel.

**Niche fit.** Lifestyle, aesthetic. Avoid for brand dumps.

**Example.** "Photo dump because I miss summer." Beach photos, sun-bleached colors, a sunset, a melting popsicle, a sandy footprint. Dropped in October.

---

## 8. "[Brand] week" / "Week [N] at [Brand]"

**Setup.** The brand version of the recap dump. Reads as a behind-the-scenes / community update rather than an ad. Works particularly well for cafes, restaurants, small studios, gyms, bookstores — places with daily texture.

**Niche fit.** Brand week-recap. Skip for solo creators.

**Example.** "Week 12 at <Cafe>." Latte art, a regular's smile, a new pastry, a chalkboard sign, a sunset through the window, the team after close. Reads as a community vlog, not an ad.

---

## 9. "POV: [scenario]"

**Setup.** The TikTok-native POV framing ported into a photo-dump. The scenario is short and aspirational ("…you live in Lisbon for a month", "…you finally took the trip"). Photos read as the inside of that POV.

**Niche fit.** Travel, aesthetic, fashion. Strong on TikTok; works on Reels.

**Example.** "POV: you live in Lisbon for a month." Tile floors, café tables, peeling-paint doorways, tram tracks, a balcony with laundry, a glass of vinho verde, the river at dusk.

---

## 10. "Currently obsessed with [topic]"

**Setup.** Curatorial. Implies the creator is in a phase or aesthetic moment, and this dump is the artifact of that obsession. Works for niches that have visual signatures (a color, a material, a city, a designer).

**Niche fit.** Aesthetic, fashion, lifestyle.

**Example.** "Currently obsessed with brown." Twelve photos all featuring brown as the dominant color — leather, coffee, brick, suede, a chocolate dessert, brown corduroy.

---

## 11. "[Verb]-ing my way through [thing]"

**Setup.** Active, present-progressive. Frames the dump as motion through an experience. "Eating my way through Mexico City." "Reading my way through winter." "Walking my way through October."

**Niche fit.** Travel, lifestyle, food.

**Example.** "Eating my way through Mexico City." 12 plates, top-down framing, varied palettes, varied textures. Closer photo: an empty plate.

---

## 12. (Silent — no opening text)

**Setup.** No text overlay. Photo 1 carries the full first beat alone, music-drop is the hook. Demands a strong photo 1 — a face, a striking color, a recognizable place — and a recognizable music bed.

**Niche fit.** Aesthetic-niche dumps with high-recognition source material (a known place, a known person), or any dump where the music IS the hook (a viral track currently doing the rounds).

**Example.** No text. Photo 1: a wide-open ocean horizon at sunrise, full-bleed. Music drops on photo 2's snap. Works because the photo + bed are each individually recognizable, and adding text would be redundant.

---

## Picking a hook — quick decision

- Personal recap of a stretch of time → **#2 ([Month] dump)** or **#4 (What my week looked like)**.
- Aesthetic mood, no specific time → **#3 (An ode to)** or **#6 (Just vibes)**.
- Travel / city → **#5 (N things I saw)** or **#9 (POV)** or **#11 (-ing my way through)**.
- Brand → **#8 ([Brand] week)**.
- Strong photo 1 + recognizable music → **#12 (silent)**.
- In doubt → **#2 ([Month] dump)**. Universal, low-risk, never feels off.
