# Hooks — Japanese Hyper-Motion Product Ad

The 0-2s pattern-interrupt that earns the next 13s of attention. This template's hook is one of the highest-density 1.8s in any format we ship: a 5-frame black flash, a color-slam, a product whip-in, a center slam with rotation-wobble, and a katakana impact-text punch — all before the music's first downbeat.

## Anatomy of the 1.8s hook (scene-01)

| Frame range | Beat | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-5f (0-0.17s) | Black flash | Pure black, no audio | Silence (pre-music tension) |
| 5-15f (0.17-0.50s) | BAMM color slam | Cadmium-yellow + hot-pink starburst BURSTS into frame | Music drum-fill sting (first downbeat) + bass-impact whoosh |
| 15-40f (0.50-1.33s) | Product whip-in | Product WHIPS in from right edge with massive hot-pink motion-blur trail | Whoosh sustained |
| 40-54f (1.33-1.80s) | Center slam + katakana | Product SLAMS dead-center, rotation-wobble, katakana 「バーン！」 punches in beside it | Second drum-sting + impact-bass on slam |

**The brand isn't even named yet.** The hook earns attention via pure pattern-interrupt energy — palette saturation, motion-blur trail, comic-burst impact text. Brand identity is delivered in scene-02 (macro mascot reveal) and locked in scene-06 (logo + slogan slam).

## Hook variants (pick by audience)

### Variant 1 — Default "BAMM color slam + whip-in" (validated)

Use when: product has strong silhouette + accent color that reads at speed.

```
EXPLOSIVE high-velocity entrance: the {{product_name}} device WHIPS in from the far right
side of the frame at extreme speed with a massive hot-pink motion-blur trail streaking
behind it across the entire frame, then SLAMS to a violent dead-stop in the dead center
with subtle screen-shake and rotation wobble. A MASSIVE yellow sunburst flash explodes
outward behind it on impact. Bold white katakana impact text 「バーン！」 PUNCHES into the
frame. Camera: snap-zoom from wide to medium-close on impact.
```

### Variant 2 — "Macro mascot zoom-out" hook

Use when: product has an iconic on-screen mascot / animated UI element that's the meme-handle (Flipper dolphin, Tamagotchi creature, Playdate's d-pad mascot).

```
EXTREME MACRO opening: a single screen pixel illuminates → rapid pixel-by-pixel typewriter
animation lights up the OLED screen revealing the [mascot] face. As the mascot fully lights
up, the camera RAPIDLY PULLS BACK at high speed, revealing the full {{product_name}} device,
which then SLAMS into hero pose with screen-shake. Bold katakana 「[mascot-name]！」 PUNCHES
in. Camera: extreme macro → snap-pull-back → static.
```

### Variant 3 — "Tile-grid floor drop" hook

Use when: product is small / cute / meme-able (Tamagotchi-class, Game Boy, mini-console).

```
Top-down view of an empty pink-and-cyan tile-grid checkerboard floor. The {{product_name}}
device DROPS into frame from above at extreme speed with motion-blur trail, BOUNCES once
on impact with a comic-style screen-shake, settles in center frame. Kawaii sparkle particles
BURST outward from the bounce point. Katakana 「[brand-kana-short]！」 PUNCHES in beside the
device. Camera: static top-down → 10% punch-in on bounce.
```

### Variant 4 — "Chibi POV pre-reveal" hook (highest kawaii energy)

Use when: target audience is anime-first, gadget-second (otaku channels, JP TikTok, kawaii-fashion crossover).

```
A 2D flat-anime chibi mascot ([chibi-a]) BURSTS into frame from below with sparkle-trail
and anime SPEED-LINES, looks at camera with anime kira-kira sparkle-eyes, then JUMPS UP
and lands on top of the {{product_name}} which appears underneath it on the tile-grid floor.
The chibi strikes a victory pose. Katakana 「ジャジャーン！」 (ta-daa!) PUNCHES in. Camera:
extreme low-angle → cuts to wide on the landing.
```

## Hook design rules

1. **No VO in the first 1.8s.** Music + SFX only. VO is not in this format anywhere — this rule is structural, not just hook-specific.
2. **Brand DOES NOT appear by name in the hook.** Mascot or product silhouette only. The kana brand reveal lives in scene-02 (macro) and scene-06 (logo slam). Hook earns the attention; brand cashes it in.
3. **Drum-fill sting on the impact frame (frame 15 + frame 40).** Pre-impact silence (frames 0-15) makes the slam land. Without the silence-then-sting beat, the music feels like wallpaper instead of choreography.
4. **Katakana impact text is mandatory.** ALL CAPS comic-burst typography with hot-pink outline. 「バーン！」 (baan! — explosion) is the default; swap for 「ガーン！」 (gaan! — shock), 「ジャジャーン！」 (ta-daa! — reveal), 「ドカン！」 (dokan! — boom) depending on the hook variant. One word, two syllables max.
5. **Hot-pink motion-blur trail is the format's signature.** Even if your product's accent isn't pink, the motion-blur should be hot-pink — it's the genre marker, not the product marker.
6. **The hook frame ends with the product CENTERED and STILL.** Whatever motion happened, frame 54 is a stable hero-pose so scene-02 can cut clean into the macro-tease. No moving exit frames.

## What does NOT work as a hook in this format

- **Slow camera moves.** A slow dolly-in over 1.8s reads as confident-narrative-ad, not hyperpop. Wrong genre.
- **Talking-head opens.** Even if you're going to include a human in scene-03, the hook is product-first. Humans in the hook diffuse the product identity.
- **Wide establishing shots of the location.** No "here's a Japanese arcade" wide. The format is intimate from frame one — the product fills the frame.
- **Captions / VO.** No caption layer in this template at all. Diegetic katakana baked into the still is the only text.
- **Aspect-ratio variation.** This format is 9:16 only. 1:1 / 16:9 versions look entirely different (less tile-grid depth, less kana punctuation impact); they're a different template, not a variant of this one.
