# Hooks — 0–2s establishing patterns

The first 2 seconds of a 75s cinematic short does the same job as the first 2 seconds of a 15s viral hyper-cut: **it tells the viewer what register they're in.** If the establishing shot reads as viral / energetic / digital, the viewer leaves before the slow piano enters. The hook for this template is **the absence of movement** — the city is asleep, the camera is locked, the music has not entered yet, and *something tiny moves*.

Six hook patterns validated against the source project + variants. Each is a 4-6s opening shot; the title card (if any) never appears in the hook — it's pure visual.

## Pattern 1 — Empty platform + single bird takeoff

The source project's shot 1. Static wide of an empty `{{city_name}}` `{{rail_system_signature}}` platform at dawn, single sparrow on the yellow tactile line. Mid-shot: the sparrow takes off, leaving the platform empty. Cumulonimbus stacks behind a power-line silhouette. Cool slate-blue grade, lifted blacks, soft halation on platform fluorescents still on.

```
Locked-off wide of an empty {{city_name}} {{rail_system_signature}} platform at dawn.
Single sparrow on the yellow tactile line in the foreground-middle. Faint cumulonimbus stacking
behind a power-line silhouette in deep background. Platform fluorescent ad-signs still on,
soft halation on the glass. Over 4-6 seconds: the sparrow shifts weight twice, then takes off
toward camera-right, leaving the platform completely empty.
```

**Why it works:** the single moving element is alive, fragile, and tiny — sets the contemplative register immediately. The takeoff is the "the city is about to wake" beat without needing words.

## Pattern 2 — Condensation-fogged window + first character introduction

A medium close-up FROM OUTSIDE a kissaten / cafe window. The lower third of the window is fogged with condensation. `{{character_a}}` is visible through the glass, mid-action of lifting a film camera toward their eye. Warm tungsten interior + cool dawn exterior = teal/orange split done subtly.

```
Static medium close-up FROM OUTSIDE a {{city_name}} kissaten window booth.
Lower third fogged with condensation. {{character_a}} sits inside, holding a film
camera in both hands, beginning to raise it toward their right eye, mouth slightly
open in quiet concentration. Foreground droplets of condensation in soft blur.
Over 5 seconds: the camera lifts to eye level, the subject pauses, presses shutter
(visual click — no audio).
```

**Why it works:** the *frame-within-a-frame* through fogged glass is the template's signature framing rule. Establishing it as the second shot (after the platform pattern) tells the viewer "everything in this piece will be seen through something."

## Pattern 3 — Neon-flash establishing wide

For variants that need a higher initial energy (e.g. Hong-Kong-vaporwave variation). A locked-off wide of a `{{city_name}}` alley or storefront at night, dominated by `{{japanese_signage_type}}` neon. One sign flickers once mid-shot — the only motion. Magenta + cyan grade with deep blacks.

```
Locked-off wide of a {{city_name}} alley at night, dominated by {{japanese_signage_type}}
neon signage stacked vertically on both walls. Wet asphalt reflects the neon in vertical
smears. Over 4 seconds: one neon sign in the upper-left flickers once, returns to steady.
No other movement in frame. Deep magenta + cyan grade, lifted blacks toward navy.
```

**Why it works:** the single flicker reads as the city *just barely alive*. The signage stack establishes the typography density that will pay off in later shots (signage typography slams on shop windows, train interiors, vending alleys).

## Pattern 4 — Pastoral memory-frame opener (the honeybee-DNA insert)

The source project's shot 0 (added in storyboard v2). WIDE of `{{character_a}}` standing alone in a vast summer field, massive cumulonimbus clouds towering behind. Small figure in landscape (~1/5 frame height). The wind crosses the field; her cardigan ripples; she does not turn.

```
Static wide of {{character_a}} standing alone in a vast summer field, facing away from camera
toward a horizon dominated by massive cumulonimbus clouds. {{character_a}} occupies ~1/5 of
the frame height — small figure in landscape. Wardrobe younger / hometown register
(faded white shirt + dark navy pleated skirt). Over 6 seconds: wind crosses the field, her
cardigan ripples once, then again, then stillness. She does not turn.
```

**Why it works:** establishes the piece as a *memory* or *dream frame* around the city arc. The pastoral opener creates a temporal ambiguity — "is this place real or remembered?" — that pays off at the half-smile close (shot 13). Strongly tied to KYO KIMURA's `honeybee` register.

## Pattern 5 — Rain-on-glass macro (cold open)

For variants where the weather binding is the headline. Extreme close-up of rain hitting a clear-plastic vinyl umbrella or a shop window. Macro-feel, shallow DoF, deep blacks, cool blue-cyan grade. NO subject in frame yet — the establishing shot is pure texture + sound-absence.

```
Macro close-up of rain hitting a clear-plastic vinyl umbrella OR a wet shop window.
Single individual drops trail down the surface; small splashes catch a faint tungsten
warm bounce from off-frame signage. No subject in frame yet. Over 5 seconds: a single
larger drop forms at the top, runs down the surface, splits into two smaller drops.
Cool blue-cyan grade, deep blacks, faint warm signage glow as the only color anchor.
```

**Why it works:** zero diegetic invariant is amplified — rain *should* have audio, but doesn't. The viewer hears the piano-bed enter against the visual of rain that should be loud. Sets the unease that the rest of the arc pays off.

## Pattern 6 — JP signage typography slam (held-frame static)

For variants where signage is the headline (e.g. dense Shimokitazawa storefront variation). A static medium of a single `{{japanese_signage_type}}` storefront sign — unreadable kanji, faded paint, slight wear — that fills 60-70% of the frame. The only movement: a single fluorescent flicker, or a passing reflection of a car's headlights crossing the sign-face.

```
Static medium of a single {{japanese_signage_type}} storefront sign (unreadable kanji,
faded paint, slight wear, slightly curled at the corner from humidity) occupying 60-70%
of the frame. Tungsten interior glow leaks from a doorway at the bottom-right. Over 5 seconds:
a single fluorescent in the sign flickers once, OR a passing reflection of a car's headlights
crosses the sign-face left-to-right.
```

**Why it works:** typography-as-protagonist. The viewer is forced to look at the signage as composition, not as text. Pays off when later shots return to this kind of frame for a beat.

## Anti-patterns (what these hooks DO NOT do)

- **DO NOT** open on a face in the first shot — the viewer needs to see the *place* first. Faces enter at shot 2 earliest.
- **DO NOT** open on movement that requires hard audio (a car horn, a train whistle, footsteps) — zero diegetic. If the visual demands audio, the absence reads as broken, not contemplative.
- **DO NOT** open with a title card — the title (if any) goes on the final fade-to-black, not the opening.
- **DO NOT** open with a camera move (pan, push-in, crane) — locked-off-tripod invariant starts at frame 0.
- **DO NOT** open with VHS lines, date stamps, or any "trashy Y2K" treatment — that's a different register entirely.
