Two worked variants showing how the slots are filled across different action archetypes. Both follow the same 10-shot structure (5 in clip 1, 5 in clip 2), AUDIO POLICY block, painterly STYLE block, and `--duck` music mix.

### What was distinctive

- Named pop-culture style reference (Musashi Miyamoto / Vagabond) locked the silhouette of character B across both clips — no drift between bowl and street scenes.
- Height delta (170cm vs 188cm) gave the camera something to work with on side-by-side framing.
- Hair-silhouette delta (white buzz cut vs crimson topknot) made characters instantly distinguishable even in low-light silhouette shots.
- Continuity anchor worked perfectly: clip 1 ends rolling out of the bowl, clip 2 opens with "JUST rolled out" using the same sky + palm phrasing — 12s+15s concat reads as continuous.

### What we'd do differently

- Skip the VS character-card poster ($0.35 + 4 min saved).
- Lock the 2-clip stitch structure on turn 1 (saves the unused $2.10 standalone duel clip).

### What's distinctive vs Variant A

- **Blue-hour magenta-and-teal palette** instead of golden-hour cobalt-and-peach — same color-block grammar, different time-of-day tone.
- **Aragorn / Strider** style reference (not Musashi / Vagabond) on character B — keeps the named-pop-culture pattern but pivots the silhouette to a Western action-fantasy register.
- **WHOOSH / SNAP / VAULT** caption words instead of KRRRACK / SLAM — same comic-SFX grammar tuned to parkour vocabulary (lighter air-displacement words vs heavier impact words).
- **Cinematic orchestral-action music** instead of skate-punk — different genre register but same NO-vocal / NO-modern-artist rule.
- **Continuity anchor pivots on a precision-jump landing** instead of a rolling exit — clip 1 ends mid-air, clip 2 opens with the landing impact.

### Hook pattern recommendation

Pattern 1 (snap-zoom-into-ink-stroke-word) is the strongest fit. Open on WHOOSH in chunky ink linework on a creamy painterly canvas, snap-zoom into the first frame of KAI mid-vault over a HVAC stack.

## What the two variants prove

- **The painterly STYLE block + AUDIO POLICY block + 10-shot structure are transferable** across very different action archetypes (skater vs parkour, golden vs blue hour, urban-ground vs rooftop-layer).
- **The named pop-culture style reference per character is the lock that prevents drift** across both clips — but the specific reference (Musashi, Aragorn, Spike, Edward Elric) is per-instance and must be authored fresh.
- **The continuity anchor is the cheapest, most reliable way to stitch two seedance clips** for a stylized scene. The anchor is in the prompt text, not in i2v frame-handoffs.
