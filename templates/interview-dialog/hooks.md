# Interview / Dialog — hook bank

Twelve opens for the first 0-3 seconds. Each is the 2-shot establish + first line. After the open, alternate turns per `prompt-cookbook.md`.

The hook does three things in 3 seconds: (1) establish the pairing visually (2-shot or split establish), (2) signal which format mode this is, (3) pose a question or claim that pulls the viewer into the rest of the dialog. Don't write hooks that resolve in the first line — leave the answer for turn 2.

---

## 1. "[Question pose] — wait, really?"

> A: "So you're saying — wait, really? You actually do this every morning?"

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview, peer-discussion.
- **Audio cue:** A's voice ends mid-sentence with audible surprise; sharp rising intonation on "really?"
- **Niche:** wellness, productivity, finance habits, cooking technique.
- **Why it lands:** the half-finished question signals an organic conversation, not a script. The viewer leans in for the answer.

## 2. "We need to talk about X."

> A: "Okay. We need to talk about [unspoken industry secret]."
> B: "Finally."

- **Format mode fit:** peer-discussion, podcast-style.
- **Audio cue:** A flat-serious; B exasperated relief.
- **Niche:** tech, beauty, finance "things insiders know."
- **Why it lands:** "we" makes the audience the third person at the table. "Finally" telegraphs that the answer is overdue and worth staying for.

## 3. "I've got [expert] here who's gonna explain X."

> A: "I've got [archetype] here, and she's about to explain why [common belief] is completely wrong."

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview.
- **Audio cue:** A enthusiastic / pitching; B silent reaction shot in 2-shot.
- **Niche:** any expert-led category — health, law, finance, engineering.
- **Why it lands:** explicit expert frame + explicit contrarian claim = high-intent click-through.

## 4. "Most people don't know this — [expert], tell them."

> A: "Most people don't know this. [B], tell them what you told me yesterday."

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview.
- **Audio cue:** A direct-to-camera then turn-to-B; B starts on cut.
- **Niche:** science explainers, industry secrets, "behind the scenes."
- **Why it lands:** the "tell them what you told me" frame implies an existing relationship — feels real, not staged.

## 5. "Quick interview with [expert]."

> A: "Quick interview with a [archetype]. Question one: [pointed question]?"

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview, podcast-style.
- **Audio cue:** A snappy, list-energy; B answers immediately on cut.
- **Niche:** rapid-fire format — anything that benefits from question/answer pacing.
- **Why it lands:** "quick" sets expectation of payoff density; numbered question signals more coming.

## 6. Split-screen: "his take vs hers."

> [Both visible, lower-third color-coded] A: "[Strong take]." B: "Absolutely not."

- **Format mode fit:** split-screen-reaction.
- **Audio cue:** A confident; B immediate cold-water rebuttal — overlap by 0.2s.
- **Niche:** relationships, lifestyle debates, "which is better" content.
- **Why it lands:** disagreement in the first 2s = stakes. Viewer wants to see who wins.

## 7. "I asked [expert] X — listen to what they said."

> A [piece-to-camera, B in 2nd shot]: "I asked a [archetype] [question]. Listen to what they said."
> [Cut to B]: B: "[Answer that breaks the assumption]."

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview, podcast-style.
- **Audio cue:** A sets up reverent; cut to B with conviction.
- **Niche:** investigative tone — health myths, finance myths, "what they don't tell you."
- **Why it lands:** the "listen to what they said" creates a 1-second pause-and-deliver — a tiny cliffhanger.

## 8. "You'll never guess what [B] just told me."

> A: "You'll never guess what [B] just told me about [topic]."
> B [cut, mild smile]: "It's not that crazy."
> A: "It's pretty crazy."

- **Format mode fit:** peer-discussion, podcast-style.
- **Audio cue:** A excited; B understated; the tension between the two energies sells it.
- **Niche:** lifestyle, food, travel reveals.
- **Why it lands:** the back-and-forth in three lines establishes the relationship, the topic, and the stakes.

## 9. "Stop saying X. Tell them why, [B]."

> A [direct]: "Stop saying [common phrase / belief]. [B], tell them why."

- **Format mode fit:** expert-interview, peer-discussion.
- **Audio cue:** A firm, almost annoyed; cut to B explaining calmly.
- **Niche:** language, etiquette, professional norms, science misconceptions.
- **Why it lands:** prescriptive open + delegated authority = high engagement on opinionated content.

## 10. Split-screen reaction to a third source.

> [Both visible, third source plays in upper third or as inset] A & B watch for 1.5s, then —
> A: "Wait — pause it."
> B: "Yeah, that's not right."

- **Format mode fit:** split-screen-reaction.
- **Audio cue:** ambient source audio for 1.5s, then both speakers cut over it.
- **Niche:** debunk videos, commentary on news / clips, "we react to X."
- **Why it lands:** the third source is the bait; the two-person reaction is the payoff structure. Triple visual interest.

## 11. "Two [archetypes], one question."

> A: "Two [archetypes], one question: [pointed question]?"
> B: "I have a strong opinion."

- **Format mode fit:** peer-discussion, split-screen-reaction.
- **Audio cue:** A neutral-introductory; B with knowing energy.
- **Niche:** "two cooks", "two designers", "two doctors weigh in."
- **Why it lands:** explicit format-frame + explicit promise of opinion = the viewer knows what they're staying for.

## 12. Podcast-style cold open: mid-thought.

> [Sit-down 2-cam, no establishing] A: "...and that's exactly when I realized — [B], you tell it."
> B [laughs, cut]: "Okay, so —"

- **Format mode fit:** podcast-style.
- **Audio cue:** start mid-laugh / mid-sentence — sounds like "we cut into a real conversation."
- **Niche:** any podcast-flavored content; storytelling, interview shows.
- **Why it lands:** in-medias-res. The viewer joined an interesting conversation already in progress and doesn't want to leave.
