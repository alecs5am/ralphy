# Hooks — tutorial-how-to

12 power-word hook patterns. Pick one. The hook is one line, ≤ 7 words, spoken in the first 1.5 seconds and burned-in on screen as the first frame.

The hook word is the single most-tested element of any Shorts tutorial. Hoyos, Inflow, and the YouTube Creator team all converge on the same data: the first 1.5 seconds determine 70%+ of average view duration. Pick the hook word first, write everything else after.

**Discipline:**
- One power word per hook. Stacking ("FREE secret AI hack") reads like spam and underperforms.
- The hook word is the first word out of the mouth, not the third. "Free dinner for $4" beats "I made dinner — for free, almost."
- The on-screen text is the same word, large, top-third of frame, lands on frame 1.
- No question hooks ("Want to save money?"). They underperform statements by 20-40% on Shorts.
- No "wait for it" hooks. Show the open loop via the foreshadow at 2-5s, not a teasing word.

---

## Category 1 — Free / Cheap

### 1. The "Free" hook

**Setup.** State that the result is free, or absurdly cheap.

**Why it works.** "Free" is the highest-CTR word in direct-response copywriting because the brain treats it as a shortcut to value. On Shorts, the search engine surfaces "free" tutorials at the top of "how to" queries.

**Formula.** `Free [thing] in [time / N steps].`

**Examples.**
- "Free ChatGPT Plus, no card."
- "Free dinner from this app."
- "Free Notion templates designers use."

### 2. The "$1" / cheap-number hook

**Setup.** State the absurdly low price.

**Why it works.** A specific number is more credible than "cheap." "$4 dinner" makes the brain calculate; "cheap dinner" makes the brain scroll.

**Formula.** `$N [thing] that [outcome].`

**Examples.**
- "$4 dinner that tastes like $40."
- "$1 hack saves my AWS bill."
- "$0.10 fix for under-eye bags."

### 3. The "Stolen / Borrowed" hook

**Setup.** Frame the technique as taken from a high-status source.

**Why it works.** Implicit social proof — somebody expensive uses this — without naming a brand and triggering the AI-slop distrust.

**Formula.** `Stole this [thing] from [credible source].`

**Examples.**
- "Stole this prompt from a $400/hr consultant."
- "Borrowed this recipe from a Michelin chef."
- "This is how my CFO friend pays $0 tax."

## Category 2 — Secret / Hidden

### 4. The "Secret" hook

**Setup.** Promise a non-obvious technique.

**Why it works.** Activates the "I want to know what others don't" loop. The viewer cannot scroll until the secret lands.

**Formula.** `The secret to [outcome] nobody [tells you / uses].`

**Examples.**
- "The secret to crispy tofu nobody talks about."
- "Secret Shortcut: Cmd-Shift-3 in any app."
- "The secret ingredient is in your pantry."

### 5. The "Hidden / Buried" hook

**Setup.** Point at a feature, setting, or fact that's deliberately hard to find.

**Why it works.** Implies effort the creator already did for you. High save-rate.

**Formula.** `Hidden [feature] inside [tool / place] that [outcome].`

**Examples.**
- "Hidden Notion view nobody uses."
- "Buried Excel function that beats VLOOKUP."
- "Hidden Sephora aisle for $3 dupes."

### 6. The "Nobody Tells You" hook

**Setup.** State the truth-they-don't-tell-you.

**Why it works.** Frames the creator as your in-the-know friend. Strong on faceless YAP videos.

**Formula.** `Nobody tells you [counter-fact about the topic].`

**Examples.**
- "Nobody tells you to season the pan, not the steak."
- "Nobody tells you Roth IRA has a backdoor."
- "Nobody tells you to apply concealer last."

---

## Category 3 — Contrarian / Wrong

### 7. The "You're Doing It Wrong" hook

**Setup.** Accuse the viewer (gently) of a common mistake. Pay it off in step 1.

**Why it works.** Pattern-interrupt: viewers feel called out and need closure. Comments are guaranteed ("I do that!"), which feeds the algorithm.

**Formula.** `You've been [verb]ing [thing] wrong.`

**Examples.**
- "You've been chopping onions wrong."
- "You've been writing prompts wrong."
- "You've been investing wrong since 2020."

### 8. The "Banned / Illegal-Feeling" hook

**Setup.** Frame the technique as edgy, gray-area, or "they don't want you to know."

**Why it works.** Curiosity + slight rebellion = scroll-killer. Be careful: do NOT make actually-illegal claims. The vibe is "feels illegal," not "is illegal."

**Formula.** `[Verb]ing [thing] like this should be illegal.`

**Examples.**
- "Saving money like this should be illegal."
- "This ChatGPT trick feels banned."
- "Cooking pasta like this would shock Italians."

---

## Category 4 — Urgency / Time

### 9. The "Speed" hook

**Setup.** Cut the time-to-result dramatically.

**Why it works.** Saves the viewer's time, and the number quantifies the saving up front.

**Formula.** `[Outcome] in [absurdly short time].`

**Examples.**
- "Dinner ready in 3 minutes."
- "Inbox to zero in 90 seconds."
- "Smooth skin in one swipe."

### 10. The "Before-You" hook

**Setup.** Trigger loss-aversion — the viewer is already losing something.

**Why it works.** Loss is a stronger driver than gain. Pairs well with finance, productivity, and AI niches.

**Formula.** `Before you [common action], do this.`

**Examples.**
- "Before you open ChatGPT, do this."
- "Before you file taxes, do this."
- "Before you wash your face, do this."

---

## Category 5 — List / Number

### 11. The "Three Things" hook

**Setup.** Promise three specific items. The number 3 outperforms 5 / 7 / 10 on Shorts retention because it matches the 3-step structure.

**Why it works.** Quantifies the open loop ("I'll watch until I see all 3"). Counters cleanly: 1/3, 2/3, 3/3.

**Formula.** `3 [things] that [outcome].`

**Examples.**
- "3 ChatGPT prompts that replaced my analyst."
- "3 ingredients, restaurant pasta."
- "3 swipes for under-eye bags."

---

## Category 6 — Niche-Specific

### 12. The "Insider Term" hook

**Setup.** Use a niche-specific term as the hook word, signaling you're inside the tribe.

**Why it works.** Filters the algorithm toward the right audience and triggers high engagement from them. Lower top-of-funnel reach, much higher retention and comments.

**Formula.** `[Niche jargon] [thing / move] that [outcome].`

**Examples.**
- *Cooking:* "Maillard hack for $4 ribeye."
- *AI tools:* "System prompt that 10x'd my agent."
- *Finance:* "Mega-backdoor Roth in two clicks."
- *Beauty:* "Slugging without the petroleum."
- *Productivity:* "Inbox-zero with a single filter."

---

## Picking the right hook

Match the hook category to the niche and intent:

| Niche | Best categories |
|---|---|
| Cooking | Free / Cheap, Speed, Three Things, Insider Term |
| Productivity | Speed, Before-You, Three Things, Wrong |
| AI tools | Stolen, Secret, Three Things, Insider Term |
| Finance | $1 / Cheap, Hidden, Wrong, Banned-feeling |
| Beauty hacks | Speed, Secret, Insider Term, $1 |

When in doubt, default to the **Three Things** hook — it composes perfectly with the 3-step structure and is the most reliable across niches.
