# Brand Story — hooks.md

> Twelve openings for the first 0-2 seconds of a brand-story film. Pick one and hold it.
> Every hook must (a) avoid revealing the brand, (b) plant an emotional question, (c) feel sensory and specific.

The pattern that fails: opening on the logo. The pattern that works: opening on a hand, a breath, a question, a contradiction — and earning the brand at the end.

---

## 1. Founder hands at work

A close-up of weathered, deliberate hands shaping something — clay, dough, fabric, code on a keyboard, solder on a circuit board. No face yet. The hands are doing the thing the brand exists to do.

- **Visual code.** Craft, intention, real labor.
- **Best for.** Artisan, manufacturing, food, creative agency, dev-tool brands.
- **Cue.** Macro lens feel, soft window light, no music yet — only the sound of the work itself.

## 2. Provocative question on a face

Single tight portrait, eyes closed or just opening. A line of voiceover in the protagonist's own voice asks an emotionally loaded question: *"What if the only thing standing between you and your work was you?"*

- **Visual code.** Vulnerability, interior life.
- **Best for.** Wellness, mental health, education, coaching, identity-driven brands.
- **Cue.** Slow zoom or held framing. No cuts in the first 2s.

## 3. Archive photograph dissolve

Open on a still photo — black and white, sepia, family album texture — held for one beat, then dissolve into a present-day version of the same scene, same place, different generation.

- **Visual code.** Heritage, continuity, time.
- **Best for.** Multi-generational brands, anniversary films, family business, heritage food.
- **Cue.** Specify "still photograph held 1s, slow dissolve to live action." Match composition between the two frames.

## 4. The mundane moment that the product makes sacred

A small, completely ordinary moment — pouring coffee, sitting down at a desk, lacing up shoes — but the camera lingers, the light is reverent. The product is in frame but not announced.

- **Visual code.** Integration, belonging, life-as-it-is.
- **Best for.** Lifestyle, wellness, daily-use software, everyday consumer goods.
- **Cue.** Golden-hour or warm practical lighting. No motion in the first beat — just stillness made beautiful.

## 5. Surprising statistic or contradiction

Black screen. A line of type appears: *"Two billion people lack access to clean water. We changed it for ten thousand."* Then cut to the proof.

- **Visual code.** Stakes, scale, accountability.
- **Best for.** Nonprofit, social enterprise, mission-driven brands, manifesto register.
- **Cue.** Real number, specific, never rounded for drama. Pair with a stark visual contrast on the cut-in.

## 6. Time-lapse transformation

A compressed visual of growth — seed to plant, blank canvas to finished work, empty room to thriving space. 1.5-2s, fast enough to register as transformation.

- **Visual code.** Patience, inevitability, potential.
- **Best for.** Tech startup, education, agriculture, construction, design agency.
- **Cue.** Single locked-off camera position. Let the change inside the frame do the work.

## 7. Two unrelated moments cut together

Two completely separate worlds inter-cut for one beat each: a scientist in a lab and a parent at a kitchen table, a line worker on a factory floor and an end customer in their living room. The viewer doesn't know yet why they are paired.

- **Visual code.** Connection across distance, supply-chain humanity.
- **Best for.** B2B with consumer impact, sustainable supply chains, healthcare, deep-tech.
- **Cue.** Match action between the two cuts (both reaching, both breathing, both starting). The connection becomes the film's premise.

## 8. The friction itself

Open on the genuine problem, with no narration. A frustrated face, a tool that doesn't work, a queue that won't move, a body that won't cooperate. Hold it long enough that the viewer feels the irritation.

- **Visual code.** Pain point, real-life mess.
- **Best for.** Problem-solution arcs, B2B, software, healthcare, finance, fitness.
- **Cue.** *Real* struggle, not dramatized suffering. The audience must recognize the feeling.

## 9. Cultural moment

The brand-relevant world at a point of celebration, ritual, or migration: a graduation, a homecoming, a protest, a wedding, a market opening. The brand is incidental in frame, the moment is everything.

- **Visual code.** Belonging, pride, cultural specificity.
- **Best for.** Diaspora brands, social mission, cultural product, hospitality, fashion.
- **Cue.** Real cultural detail — the wrong detail will kill the hook faster than anything else. Reference required.

## 10. Tactile sensory open

Macro photography or extreme close-up of a *texture* — fabric weave, wood grain, water bead on a surface, steam off a cup, the rough side of a leather strap. Paired with a single ambient sound (wood scrape, water drip, breath).

- **Visual code.** Materiality, presence, slowness.
- **Best for.** Premium product, food, fashion, hospitality, design tools.
- **Cue.** Sound first, image one frame later. Audio is doing as much work as visuals.

## 11. Silhouette / backlit figure

A backlit figure — gender, age, identity all unreadable — moving through a doorway, a horizon, a hallway of light. The viewer projects themselves into the silhouette.

- **Visual code.** Universality, possibility, "this could be you."
- **Best for.** Manifesto, anthem, mission-driven, finance / insurance / category-creator brands.
- **Cue.** Avoid this hook if your brand is about a *specific* hero. It works best when the protagonist is the audience.

## 12. Continuity / mid-action open

Open as if the camera caught the scene already in motion — a conversation already underway, a process already mid-step, a journey already on the road. No establishing shot.

- **Visual code.** Lived-in world, deeper story implied.
- **Best for.** Brands with rich existing world (restaurant, studio, community, working environment), behind-the-scenes register.
- **Cue.** Dialogue or sound effect across the cut from black — voice continues from before the camera arrived.

---

## Choosing rules

1. **One hook per film.** Stacking two reads as music-video pastiche, not story.
2. **Match hook to register.** Manifesto needs hook 5 / 11. Heritage needs hook 3 / 4. Problem-solution needs hook 8. Don't open a wellness film on a contradiction-statistic — wrong key.
3. **Hook and ending should rhyme.** A film that opens on the founder's hands should end on those same hands, now finished, holding the result. A film that opens on a question should answer it (or reframe it) in the closing reflection. The rhyme is what makes the ending land.
4. **Cut the hook one beat shorter than feels safe.** Almost every brand story tested too long on the open. 2 seconds is plenty.
